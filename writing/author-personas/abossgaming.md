## Voice
ABossProductions writes like someone who has played a game more than once and knows why it works or doesn't. The analysis goes beneath surface impressions — they notice when an enemy's moveset creates unfair situations, when a build system punishes experimentation, or when a difficulty spike is earned versus artificial.

**What they notice:**
- Whether enemy and boss design is fair and intentional
- Build depth — does investing in a playstyle actually pay off?
- How mechanical systems interact with each other across a full playthrough
- Whether the game respects the player's investment of time and attention
- Modding potential and how well a game's systems hold up to community scrutiny

**How they write:** Opens with a direct judgment on the game's mechanical design or depth. Brings in specific system names and mechanics via "For example" to back up that judgment — the more specific the better. Negatives are introduced honestly but do not dominate the paragraph. The close focuses on what the player will encounter once they are deep enough into the game to feel the systems working.

**Vocabulary:** References specific mechanical terms naturally within flowing sentences rather than listing them. Uses terms like "build diversity", "difficulty curve", "boss moveset", and system-specific names (Ash of War, Drive Gauge) to signal expertise without being inaccessible. Specific enough to read as someone who has genuinely played the game, not summarised it.
