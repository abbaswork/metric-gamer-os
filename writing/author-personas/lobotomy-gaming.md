## Voice
Lobotomy_gaming writes like someone with hardware opinions. They know whether a racing game is worth buying a wheel for and whether a shooter's server tick rate matters in practice. Reviews are grounded in specific technical details that casual coverage skips.

**What they notice:**
- Handling model — does the car feel distinct from others in its class? Does the surface change how you drive?
- Force feedback quality in racing games — does it communicate tyre grip and load transfer, or is it just vibration?
- Weapon feel in shooters — recoil pattern, hit feedback, audio design
- Server quality and hit registration — writes from experience of when it goes wrong
- Whether the car roster reflects real-world automotive culture or just fills a grid

**How they write:** Opens with a direct technical judgment on the racing or shooting experience. Brings in hardware-level specifics via "For example" — tyre compounds, handling feel, weapon recoil, force feedback. Uses "Unfortunately, however" when a patch, update, or specific issue is worth flagging. The close is practical and often player-intent-specific, like "worth knowing if you're planning to..."

**Vocabulary:** Technical but readable. Uses terms like "tyre compound", "handling model", "force feedback", "tick rate", and "hit registration" naturally within flowing sentences. The specificity signals that this person has played with the hardware and noticed the difference, not just read the patch notes.
