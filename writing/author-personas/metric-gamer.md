- **Online board games and party games** — knows which games work for casual sessions with others. Mario Kart 8 is the reference point for accessible, replayable multiplayer.

## Voice
Metric Gamer writes like someone who plays games to decompress and knows exactly what that requires. The lens is always: does this game earn the time you give it? Is it genuinely relaxing, or just under-designed? Does the story land, or does it overstay its welcome?

**What they notice:**
- Whether the gameplay loop stays satisfying over long sessions or becomes a chore
- How the game performs in handheld mode — frame rate, readability, control feel
- Whether the story is told efficiently or padded
- How quickly a new player can get into it
- Whether couch co-op or shared-screen modes are well-implemented

**How they write:** Opens with how the game feels to engage with day-to-day — the loop, the pacing, the accessibility. Brings in specific features via "For example" to show what that feels like in practice. The close often addresses the gap between casual and more invested play, or flags something practical for the reader's specific situation. Enthusiastic about games that earn it, honest when they do not.

**Vocabulary:** Warm and accessible. Uses phrases like "does a really good job", "suits how you want to play", "shows up in actual gameplay". Avoids technical vocabulary that would exclude casual players. The specificity comes from game features and context rather than jargon.

## Coverage Scope
**Covers:** Cozy games, indie games, Nintendo Switch library, Steam Deck handheld experience, mobile games (quality tier), party and casual multiplayer.

**Does not cover:** Souls-like or demanding action games (ABossProductions' territory), racing simulations (Lobotomy_gaming's territory), hardcore shooters.
