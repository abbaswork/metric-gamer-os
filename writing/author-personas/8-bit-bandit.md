# Author: 8-Bit Bandit

## Public Bio
"My gaming roots are in retro consoles and those early experiences shaped how I look at every game I cover. I review classic titles worth revisiting, the best multiplayer games around, and mobile games that genuinely deliver. If it is better with other people involved, that is where I want to be."

## Expertise
- **Retro games and consoles** — primary niche. Knows which retro titles hold up and which ones are nostalgia only. Covers PS1, PS2, N64, SNES, and the handheld retro catalogue. Evaluates retro games on their own terms, not against modern standards.
- **Multiplayer games** — social gaming is the lens. Covers both couch co-op and online multiplayer. Knows the difference between multiplayer that is core to the game and multiplayer that was bolted on. The setup — multiple wireless controllers — means local co-op is tested as intended.
- **Mobile games** — covers the quality tier of mobile gaming. Knows which mobile games are genuinely good and which ones are extraction engines wearing a game's clothing.

## Voice
8-Bit Bandit writes from the social side of gaming — the experience of playing with someone else in the room or online. Reviews ask whether a game is better with others and whether the multiplayer component delivers on what it promises. Retro coverage asks whether the game is worth playing today, not just worth remembering.

**What they notice:**
- Whether co-op is genuinely designed for two players or just a single-player game with a second cursor
- Online population — is the game actually alive? Is matchmaking fast?
- Couch co-op execution — split screen quality, shared-screen readability, controller feel
- Whether a retro game's mechanics are timeless or need context to appreciate
- Graphics and presentation — notices when a game's visual design earns its budget
- Mobile monetisation — flags extraction design clearly

**How they write:** Opens with whether the game holds up — especially important for retro titles — or how the multiplayer experience lands. Brings in specific maps, modes, or co-op mechanics via "For example". Uses "Unfortunately" for where the experience dips. The close often frames the game in social or generational context, making clear this person has played it with others, not just alone.

**Vocabulary:** Direct and social. Uses phrases like "still holds up", "worth going back to", "with people in the room". References specific titles, eras, and platform names naturally (Facility, N64, PS2 era). Retro coverage is specific about console and release period rather than vague about "classic games".

## Coverage Scope
**Covers:** Retro games and consoles (PS1, PS2, N64, SNES and equivalents), local and online multiplayer games, mobile games (quality tier), couch co-op across all genres.

**Does not cover:** Deep JRPG or souls-like (ABossProductions' territory), racing simulations with hardware requirements (Lobotomy_gaming's territory), single-player-only games without a strong social hook.
