## Introduction

The Finals is the free-to-play team shooter from Embark Studios that most players in the FPS space pointed to as the genre's most interesting new arrival when it launched on PC in December 2023. It's built around three-player squads and a destruction engine that lets teams collapse buildings, breach through floors, and reshape the arena mid-match. Extended to PS5 and Xbox Series X|S a year later, it had crossed 40 million registered players within its first year. It sits around 50,000 concurrent Steam players as of mid-2026 and is free to play across all current platforms.

Whether it still earns that attention depends on what you're after. The core design is distinctive and the destruction layer genuinely changes how squad play works, but the cheating situation in ranked has been the game's most significant ongoing problem since Season 5, and both of those things are worth understanding before you invest. The metric breakdown below covers gunplay, movement, squad design, maps, game modes, community health, and the monetisation model so you have a clear picture of what the game delivers in 2026.

## Metrics

### Gunplay — 4 / 5

Picking your loadout in The Finals means picking how your class plays. A Medium running the AKM is committing to a controlled, mid-range engagement. The recoil is learnable and the damage rewards staying in the fight long enough to use it. A Light with an SMG is built to get in close and leave before the fight settles. The weapon follows from how the class plays, so switching class changes how a fight opens. Hit feedback is clear and the audio confirms contact, so you know what's landing without checking the kill feed. Unfortunately, Embark has adjusted recoil patterns across multiple seasons and the changes have been disruptive enough to make investing time in a specific weapon feel risky. It's worth checking the current patch state before committing to learning a weapon.

---

### Movement — 4 / 5

The base movement in The Finals is clean. The Light class has a dash and grapple hook for vertical options, and vaulting and climbing are responsive. The movement layer worth learning comes from destruction. Once you know which floors, walls, and ceilings come down and how fast, you're finding routes that players without that knowledge can't take. A Medium who breaches through a floor to reach a cashout from below is using a line that didn't exist at the start of the round, and the team defending the stairs has no answer for it. That kind of environmental reading has its own skill ceiling. Unfortunately, if Apex or Titanfall 2 is your benchmark for movement depth, the base traversal won't get there. There's no wall-running or momentum chaining, and the destruction traversal is a slower skill to develop. It's worth knowing if you're coming in expecting that layer.

---

### Squad Play & Class Design — 5 / 5

Each class in The Finals fills a job the other two can't do, and a squad missing one is playing a harder version of the game. Without a Medium's healing beam, engagements that should be winnable end in the first exchange because nobody's topping health up. A Heavy can blast a new entry point into a defended position instead of pushing the doorway everyone's watching, and that ability to reshape the arena mid-fight is something a squad without one can't run. Stacking the class that looks individually strongest is a mistake you feel in ranked Cashout before the first round ends, because the squad that planned its composition is running plays yours can't match.

---

### Maps — 4 / 5

In The Finals you don't choose which arena you're playing, so map knowledge across the full rotation matters. Seoul's vertical layout is where the Light's grapple hook pays off most, with rooftops and upper floors creating angles that ground-level play can't cover. Monaco is denser and lower, where destruction mostly removes cover and engagements collapse into closer range. The destruction layer means that knowledge has a dynamic element: routes and cover that were there in round one can be rubble by round three, and knowing which structures fall fast and where fallback positions form once they do is a genuine edge. Unfortunately, some cashout locations in the rotation have a defender advantage that's been present long enough to be well understood and still hasn't been fixed. If you hit one of those spots on offence in ranked, playing for the ring-out is usually the better call.

---

### Game Modes — 2 / 5

Cashout has a structure no other active free shooter has matched. Three teams compete over a vault, trying to bank it before the others steal it back, while the arena breaks apart around the fight. Power Shift, the objective-push mode, plays at a different tempo and has its own identity. The problem is what's been removed. Terminal Attack was the game's 5v5 mode with no respawns, where a single mistake ended your round and the tactical read mattered as much as the mechanical one. It's been gone since Season 8, and so has Bank It, leaving a shorter mode list than the game launched with. Beyond the mode removals, there's no offline option. The training range requires a live connection and the only targets available are stationary dummies that don't move at the pace any class actually plays. If you want to work on mechanics outside a live match, the game doesn't give you a way to do it.

---

### Community — 2 / 5

Getting into a match in The Finals is fast. The game sits around 50,000 concurrent Steam players as of July 2026 and matchmaking at Platinum and above comes in under 90 seconds. The trouble is in the ranked mode. Since Season 5 it's had an active cheating problem in NA, with aimbots and wallhacks appearing often enough that a clean ranked session is the exception. The game's anti-cheat hasn't resolved it. Casual Cashout draws a different player pool and is more tolerable, but if ranked is where you plan to spend your time, the cheating is the reason to think twice before investing.

---

### Free to Play — 4 / 5

The Finals sits on the fairer end of the free-to-play shooter spectrum. All three classes are available from your first session and every weapon unlocks through play. New weapons join the free pool when they're added, not locked behind a seasonal paywall. No paid blueprint comes with a different reticle or a better stat line. That's been a real issue in other free shooters, and The Finals doesn't have it. The one caveat is the Ultimate tier at $29.99, which includes a 25% XP boost for the full season. It doesn't lock any competitive content, but free players earn progression at a slower pace, and if you're starting mid-season the gap is noticeable. The core competitive game is fully accessible without spending, which is the actual test for any free shooter.

## Pros

- Destruction isn't cosmetic — breaching a floor or collapsing a ceiling changes the fight mid-round and requires real squad planning to use
- All three classes and every weapon are free from the first session, with no competitive content locked behind a paywall
- Cashout has a structure no other free shooter has matched, with three teams competing over a vault while the arena breaks apart around the fight
- Crossplay across PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S draws from a larger matchmaking pool at every rank

## Cons

- The ranked mode in NA has had an active cheating problem since Season 5 that the anti-cheat system hasn't resolved
- Terminal Attack and Bank It have both been removed, leaving a shorter active mode list than the game launched with
- Recoil changes across multiple seasons make investing time in a specific weapon feel risky
- The training range requires a live connection and only offers stationary dummies, with no way to work on mechanics outside a live match

## Who Is This Game For?

**Play this if** you want a free shooter built around a class system where each role fills a gap the other two can't, you have a regular three-person squad to play with, and you're going into ranked with clear expectations about the current cheating situation in NA.

**Skip this if** ranked integrity is a dealbreaker and NA is your region, if you want a mode list with depth beyond Cashout and Power Shift, or if offline practice tools are part of how you develop.

---

## Verdict

The Finals has a core design that sets it apart from every other free shooter available — the destruction, the class system, and Cashout are all things without a close equivalent. The problem is that ranked in NA has had a cheating problem since Season 5 that hasn't been resolved, and ranked is where the squad design is most rewarding. If you're going in with a regular three-player squad and realistic expectations about the current state of competitive play, the core game is worth the time.

## FAQs

**Is The Finals cross platform?**
Yes, The Finals supports crossplay across PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S, enabled by default.

**What platforms is The Finals available on?**
The Finals is available on PC via Steam, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S. A PS4 version launched in December 2024 but was shut down in March 2026.

**How long is The Finals?**
There's no campaign, so there's no set completion time. Individual Cashout matches run around 15 to 20 minutes. How long you play overall depends on what you're working toward in ranked or the seasonal battle pass.

**Is The Finals hard?**
The entry point is accessible — all three classes are available immediately and the destruction basics take a match or two to pick up. The skill ceiling in ranked comes from knowing the destruction layer, playing your class role in a coordinated squad, and dealing with the cheating situation in North American lobbies.

**Can you play The Finals solo?**
You can queue solo and be matched with random teammates, but the game is designed around three-player coordination. A Heavy who doesn't know how to use wall demolition with a squad behind it is a less effective version of what the class can do with coordination.

**Can you play The Finals with friends online?**
Yes, you can squad up with up to two friends and queue into Cashout or Power Shift. Crossplay means platform doesn't matter.

**Is The Finals free?**
Yes, The Finals is free to download and play on all current platforms. All three classes and every weapon are available without spending.

**Is The Finals pay to win?**
No. All classes and weapons are in the free pool from day one. The Ultimate battle pass tier at $29.99 includes a 25% XP boost for the season, which gives paying players a faster progression pace but no direct competitive advantage.

**Does The Finals have a battle pass?**
Yes. There's a standard pass and an Ultimate tier at $29.99. Both are cosmetic-focused, with the Ultimate tier adding a 25% XP boost alongside the cosmetic rewards.
