## Introduction

Gran Turismo 7 is the 2022 entry that the sim racing community treats as Polyphony Digital's full return to form after Sport's more stripped-down, online-first approach. It launched with over 400 cars and has grown to 570 across more than 70 manufacturers, racing across 41 track locations and well over 100 layouts. It's the eighth mainline entry and thirteenth game in the Gran Turismo series, and GT Sophy, Sony AI's reinforcement-learning racing AI, made its console debut here.

That reputation raises a real question for anyone deciding whether to buy in today: does the driving model live up to the hype when the AI you actually race day to day and the economy around it have both drawn real criticism. This page breaks down the physics and force feedback, track authenticity, AI opponents including where GT Sophy fits in, multiplayer, and the mod situation, so you know exactly what you're getting into before you commit to a PlayStation ecosystem built around this game.

## Metrics

### Physics, Handling & Force Feedback — 4/5

Gran Turismo 7's driving model backs up its reputation as one of the best pure driving experiences on a console. Weight transfer comes through before a mistake happens rather than after it, and different vehicle classes drive like distinctly different cars, not reskins of the same physics base. For example, a road car pushes wide through a fast corner in a way a race-spec GT3 car simply doesn't, and you can feel tire grip build and fade through a corner via wheel FFB long before the tires actually let go. Force feedback support natively covers Fanatec's CSL Elite and Podium lines, Logitech's G29 and G923, and Thrustmaster's T300RS, T-GT, and T248. Unfortunately, that support stops there: Moza, Simucube, and Simagic direct-drive bases have no official PS5 handshake, and the workaround adapters that exist leave the wheel ID unrecognised and the feedback disconnected from the in-game sliders. If you're buying a wheel specifically to pair with GT7, stick to one of the three officially supported brands rather than betting on a third-party adapter working properly.

---

### Track Authenticity & Variety — 4/5

Gran Turismo 7's track list earns its reputation on the strength of its laser-scanned real-world venues. The Nürburgring Nordschleife is built from vehicle-mounted and drone-based scan data that captures the actual camber and undulation of the circuit, and that same scanning process runs across the roster's 41 locations and well over 100 layouts. Road courses and street circuits carry that fidelity across the board, which is where the roster's real strength sits. Unfortunately, the oval side of the roster doesn't hold to the same standard: aside from Daytona itself, GT7's ovals are fictional stand-ins built to resemble real circuits rather than scans of them, with Blue Moon Bay reading as an unlicensed Pocono and Northern Isles as an unlicensed Martinsville. If oval racing specifically is what you're after, temper your expectations for the same real-world accuracy the road courses deliver.

---

### AI Opponent Quality — 2/5

Gran Turismo 7's default AI is exactly the sore point that keeps coming up: the pace is rubber-banded rather than tied to a fixed skill level. GT Café events and License tests use a "catch the rabbit" format where the AI surges hard on the straights when you're closing in, then barely defends once you've actually made the pass, so a gap you build rarely stays earned. GT Sophy, the reinforcement learning AI trained by Sony AI on car control, tactics, and etiquette, is a real exception to this and races with proper racecraft instead of scripted catch-up pace. Unfortunately, Sophy lives in Quick Race, a PS5-exclusive mode whose vehicle coverage only expanded to 340 cars with the Sophy 2.0 update, so it's not the AI most players are actually racing through the campaign. If competitive-feeling solo racing matters to you, look specifically for Sophy-supported cars and tracks rather than assuming the rest of the game's AI plays the same way.

---

### Multiplayer & League Ecosystem — 4/5

Gran Turismo 7 gives online racing real structure through Sport mode. Driver Rating and Sportsmanship Rating gate which lobby you land in, so races stay reasonably clean once you've built a track record, and the Manufacturer Cup and Nations Cup are official FIA-certified championships that feed into the Gran Turismo World Series with real prize money on the line. That FIA backing is a credential few racing games can claim. Unfortunately, structured competition beyond those Cups mostly has to be organised yourself or found through third-party communities like SimGrid, since GT7 doesn't have an in-game league directory anywhere near the scale of the genre's most dedicated platforms. If FIA-certified competition is what you're chasing, Sport mode delivers it directly. If you want a wider spread of leagues across disciplines, expect to look outside the game itself.

---

### Mod Ecosystem & Community Content — 1/5

Gran Turismo 7 is a fully closed platform, and that's true by design rather than by oversight. Every car, track, and event comes from Polyphony Digital directly, with no user-submitted content of any kind, not even the fan-made custom tracks older GT titles technically supported through hacked save data. GT Auto's livery editor and tuning shop go deep, letting you build wide-body kits, custom liveries, and engine swaps once you hit Collector Level 50, but that's an official toolset, not a community one. Unfortunately, if what you actually want is the kind of track or car mod that fills a gap the developer never addressed, GT7 has no path to that at all. If community-built content is part of what you're looking for in a racing game, you'll need to look at a PC-based sim instead, since nothing you build or download here goes further than what's built into the game itself.

## Pros

- DualSense haptic feedback communicates road texture and tire slip even without a wheel, giving pad players real information most console racers don't bother simulating
- Two-player split-screen, unlocked after GT Café Menu 9, is a rare feature in a genre that's mostly dropped local multiplayer
- Regular free updates have added dozens of tracks and well over a hundred cars since launch at no extra cost

## Cons

- The game requires 215GB of storage, a real consideration even on a PS5 SSD
- Progression is grindy: some of the best cars cost upward of 450,000 Credits, and prompts to buy Credits with real money haven't gone away
- Even with the Driver Rating and Sportsmanship Rating system sorting lobbies, Sport mode still sees its share of contact-heavy drivers in practice

## Who Is This Game For?

**Play this if** you want the deepest car-collecting sim on PlayStation, with 570 cars across more than 70 manufacturers and a livery and tuning system deep enough to make every garage your own. **Play this if** you already own a Fanatec, Logitech, or Thrustmaster wheel, since GT7's force feedback is built specifically around that hardware. **Play this if** FIA-certified competition matters to you, since the Manufacturer and Nations Cups connect Sport mode to real prize money and real-world motorsport recognition.

**Skip this if** you're mainly after single-player racing that feels legitimately competitive, since the default GT Café and License test AI is rubber-banded and GT Sophy isn't the AI you'll actually race most of the time. **Skip this if** you own a Moza, Simucube, or Simagic wheel, since none of them have official PS5 support in GT7. **Skip this if** you want to build custom tracks or download community-made cars, since the platform doesn't allow any user-submitted content.

---

## Verdict

Gran Turismo 7 is one of the best pure driving experiences on PlayStation, and paired with a supported wheel the physics and force feedback justify that reputation on their own. The AI you race day to day doesn't live up to the same standard, and the game still gates its best cars behind a grind that microtransactions are positioned to shortcut. If you're playing mainly for the driving feel, the car collection, and Sport mode's structured competition, GT7 delivers; if solo racing against genuinely fair opponents is your main draw, temper your expectations.

## FAQs

**Is Gran Turismo 7 cross platform?**
Gran Turismo 7 only shipped on PS4 and PS5, so there's no crossplay with Xbox or PC to speak of. PS4 and PS5 owners do race together in the same Sport mode lobbies and custom rooms.

**What platforms is Gran Turismo 7 available on?**
Gran Turismo 7 is available on PlayStation 4 and PlayStation 5, with PSVR2 support added later through a free update.

**How long is Gran Turismo 7?**
Working through the GT Café menus takes around 20 to 28 hours. Add in extra events and licenses and you're looking at closer to 26.5 hours, while chasing full completion runs to around 34.5 hours.

**Is Gran Turismo 7 hard?**
Gran Turismo 7 offers a wide range of driving assists and difficulty settings, so it's approachable for newcomers with them turned on. The Gold License tests and the toughest Sport mode lobbies get genuinely demanding once those assists come off.

**Does Gran Turismo 7 have multiplayer?**
Yes. Sport mode covers ranked online racing with Daily Races and FIA-certified championships, and custom Lobbies support up to 16 players for casual online races.

**Does Gran Turismo 7 have couch co-op or split screen?**
Yes, up to two players can race split-screen locally, though it's locked behind GT Café Menu 9 until you complete it.

**Can you play Gran Turismo 7 solo?**
Yes, the entire GT Café campaign, License tests, and Circuit Experience are built for solo play. Only Sport mode and Lobbies require going online.

**Do I need to play the previous Gran Turismo games first?**
No, Gran Turismo 7 doesn't require any prior entry to follow. It draws on the series' car-collecting identity but explains its systems from scratch.

**Does Gran Turismo 7 have DLC?**
No, not in the traditional expansion sense. All major content updates, including new cars and tracks, have been free, though the game does sell in-game Credits for real money to speed up progression.

**Does Gran Turismo 7 run well on PS4?**
Yes, but the PS5 version is the stronger experience, with faster loading, higher resolution and frame rate options, and DualSense haptic feedback that the PS4 version doesn't have.
