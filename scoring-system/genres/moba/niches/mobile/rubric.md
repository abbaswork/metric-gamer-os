# Metric: Mobile Experience
**Genre:** MOBA
**Niche:** Mobile
**Replaces:** Combat Feel
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = a touchscreen-native experience where the mobile platform feels like the intended way to play, 1 = a PC MOBA poorly ported to touch with unacceptable performance trade-offs

## Why This Matters

Mobile MOBAs are a distinct platform experience, not a shrunk-down version of their PC counterparts. Honor of Kings "pioneered mobile-native design by building the game from the ground up for touch-based interfaces" — dual virtual joysticks for movement and targeting, swipe mechanics for skills, contextual buttons that appear and fade to minimise screen clutter. Mobile Legends "focuses on fast-paced, aggressive action, where matches sometimes end within eight minutes" — the session length is designed around mobile play patterns, not PC MOBA conventions.

When this works, the mobile experience is the intended experience. When it doesn't, the mobile platform becomes a constant friction source: imprecise touch inputs that misfire abilities in critical moments, match lengths that extend beyond mobile session norms and punish players who need to stop mid-game, UI that becomes unreadable under the visual pressure of a team fight on a phone screen, and performance issues that cause frame drops on mid-range hardware — "I did notice some freezing and framerate drops in a few of my matches" (GosuGamers, Honor of Kings). A mobile MOBA that requires flagship hardware or a 40-minute commitment per session is failing the platform it shipped on.

Combat Feel is replaced by Mobile Experience in this niche because on a touchscreen, the control scheme IS the combat feel — the quality of touch input, layout customization, and how the game responds to the physical constraints of mobile are inseparable from whether combat feels good to play.

- **bittopup.com — Honor of Kings Controller Support 2025:** Honor of Kings "pioneered mobile-native design" with dual virtual joysticks and "smart contextual buttons that dynamically appear or fade to minimize screen clutter" — mobile-native design vs port is the primary quality axis
- **lootbar.gg — Mobile Legends vs Honor of Kings:** "Mobile Legends is more accessible for casual players and those new to the MOBA genre with mobile-optimized controls and streamlined mechanics"; "matches sometimes end within eight minutes" — session length optimisation as a mobile design criterion
- **research.md — Google Trends:** mobile legends pc (1, +20%); pokemon unite ios (1, +80%) — players actively seeking cross-platform access, confirming mobile-specific experience quality is a tracked concern

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Touch controls feel designed from the ground up for a touchscreen — dual virtual joystick precision, full layout customization, smart contextual buttons that minimise screen clutter under team-fight conditions. Match length targets 15–20 minutes, fitting mobile session norms without exceptions. Performance is stable on mid-range devices without excessive heat or battery drain. The UI remains legible on phone-sized screens during fast-paced play. Controller support is available for players who want it. | Playing on your phone feels natural, not compromised. The mobile experience is the intended experience. |
| 4 | Controls are well-adapted with minor friction. Layout customization is available. Most matches run 15–25 minutes. Performance is solid on most current devices with occasional frame drops on low-end hardware. UI is legible in most situations though some UI density creates readability issues in the most chaotic moments. | Mobile play works well. A few minor adaptation issues don't define the experience. |
| 3 | Controls function but feel like an adaptation rather than native design — button sizes are small, layout options are limited, or certain mechanics require precision difficult to achieve consistently on a touchscreen. Match length extends to 25–35 minutes with some frequency. Performance issues appear on mid-range devices during demanding scenes. | Playable on mobile but you're aware of the touch limitations when the controls don't respond as intended. The experience works but doesn't feel built for this platform. |
| 2 | Touch controls are a clear compromise — imprecise inputs, frequent accidental ability misfires, layout that can't be meaningfully customized. Match length is unpredictable and extends long enough that mobile sessions feel punishing when interrupted. Performance issues are common on non-flagship hardware, with regular frame drops during team fights. UI becomes cluttered and difficult to parse on a phone screen. | Mobile is a second-class experience. The game works better somewhere else. |
| 1 | Touch controls make the game barely playable — inputs are unreliable, layout is not customizable, and several mechanics are effectively inaccessible on a touchscreen. Performance issues are severe and frequent on typical mobile hardware. Match lengths mirror PC equivalents with no concession to mobile session patterns. | This is a PC game running on a phone. The mobile experience is broken. |
