# Metric: Roster Depth
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Classic
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = vast roster with a distinct champion for every playstyle, 1 = thin roster where play converges to a small viable set

## Why This Matters

In classic MOBAs, the roster is the diversity engine. League of Legends' approach to champion design is built around this: "Having a variety of healthy champions that are viable picks does several nice things for the game, including helping to prevent player burnout through the ability to swap champions from game to game" (Riot Games dev blog). When the same small cluster of picks dominates every session, the game stops feeling fresh regardless of how good the moment-to-moment gameplay is.

The research backs this up at the player level: "I get to be cool, cute characters, that have very compelling stories and are a blast." A champion is not just a mechanical toolkit; it is a personality and aesthetic a player invests in over hundreds of hours. Roster depth serves two functions simultaneously: keeping individual sessions varied, and giving players a long-term identity project within the game.

The draft phase only functions as a meaningful strategic layer when the roster is large and varied enough that bans remove outliers rather than leaving only 10 viable options across 5 positions. A shallow roster also accelerates meta staleness: when the entire viable pool is known, balance patches have less room to breathe.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 80+ champions across all roles, each with a distinct mechanical identity and playstyle. Every lane and position has multiple viable options at most skill levels. New champion additions expand underrepresented archetypes rather than duplicating existing ones. The draft phase creates genuine strategic variety because strong picks exist in every role. Lore and character personality are developed enough that players form long-term attachments to specific champions. | No matter how many hours you put in, there are champions you haven't mastered. The roster feels like a game in itself. |
| 4 | 50-80 champions with strong role coverage. Some archetypes are deeper than others but no position feels starved for choice. Draft diversity is meaningful. A few new additions feel redundant but most expand the pool. Character identity is strong for the core roster even if newer additions have thinner lore. | Strong champion variety. You'll find favourites but the roster has plenty of room to explore. |
| 3 | 30-50 champions. Most positions have 2-3 solid options but depth thins quickly outside the most popular archetypes. The draft is functional but experienced players cycle through viable picks rapidly. A clear tier of dominant champions is already visible. | Adequate roster. You have choices, but experienced players converge on the same picks regardless. |
| 2 | 15-30 champions. Clear gaps in some positions with only 1-2 viable options per role. Playstyle variety is limited. Newer additions haven't meaningfully expanded what is actually playable at a competitive level. | The roster feels repetitive within weeks. Meta pressure limits your choices more than personal preference does. |
| 1 | Fewer than 15 champions. The roster cannot support competitive draft diversity or meaningful role coverage. Play converges to a very small viable set. Character identity is shallow or underdeveloped. | Character choice is cosmetic, not strategic. The same few picks appear every game. |
