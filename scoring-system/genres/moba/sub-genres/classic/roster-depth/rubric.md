# Metric: Roster Depth
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Classic
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = vast, diverse roster with a distinct champion for every playstyle, 1 = thin roster where play converges to a small viable set

## Why This Matters

In classic MOBAs, the roster is the diversity engine. League of Legends at 170+ champions, Dota 2 at 125+ heroes — the breadth of the pool determines how many distinct gameplay experiences the game can deliver across its lifetime. The research identifies character identity as a core appeal driver: "I get to be cool, cute characters, that have very compelling stories and are a blast." A champion is not just a mechanical toolkit; it is a personality, an aesthetic, a playstyle that a player invests in over hundreds of hours.

The draft phase — the ban/pick sequence before every ranked game — only functions as a meaningful strategic layer when the roster is large and varied enough that meaningful choices exist at every position. Bans remove genuine outliers, not the only options. When the roster thins, the draft becomes a formality: the same 10–15 champions rotate through every lobby, and the strategic pre-game collapses. A deep roster also means the meta can shift between patches without old champions becoming permanently obsolete — there is always a cohort of viable picks at every position the patch cycle hasn't touched.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 80+ champions across all roles, each with a distinct mechanical identity and playstyle. Every lane and position has multiple viable options at most skill levels. New champion additions expand underrepresented archetypes rather than duplicating existing ones. The draft phase creates genuine strategic variety — bans matter because strong picks exist in every role and position. Lore and character personality are developed enough that players form long-term attachments to specific champions. | No matter how many hours you put in, there are champions you haven't mastered. The roster feels like a game in itself. |
| 4 | 50–80 champions with strong role coverage. Some archetypes are deeper than others but no position feels starved for choice. Draft diversity is meaningful. A few new additions feel redundant but most expand the pool. Character identity is strong for the core roster even if newer additions have thinner lore. | Strong champion variety. You'll find favourites but the roster has plenty of room to explore. |
| 3 | 30–50 champions. Most positions have 2–3 solid options but depth thins quickly outside the most popular archetypes. The draft is functional but experienced players cycle through viable picks rapidly. A "solved" tier of strong champions is already visible. | Adequate roster. You have choices, but experienced players converge on the same picks. |
| 2 | 15–30 champions. Clear gaps in some positions — certain roles have 1–2 viable options. Playstyle variety is limited. Newer additions haven't meaningfully expanded what's playable at a competitive level. | The roster feels repetitive within weeks. Meta pressure limits your choices more than personal preference does. |
| 1 | Fewer than 15 champions. The roster cannot support competitive draft diversity or meaningful role coverage. Play converges to a very small viable set. Character identity is shallow or underdeveloped. | Character choice is cosmetic, not strategic. The same few picks appear every game. |
