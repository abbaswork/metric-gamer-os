# Metric: Itemisation & Build Depth
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Classic
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = deep, flexible item system that rewards live strategic adaptation per game, 1 = scripted build paths per champion with no meaningful in-game decisions

## Why This Matters

Itemisation is the strategic layer that separates classic MOBAs from every other sub-genre. Hero brawlers and third-person MOBAs either remove items entirely or use significantly simplified systems. The Google Trends data signals how deeply players engage with it: "anti shield item lol" at +450% search interest shows players mid-game searching for specific counter-item decisions, not following a pre-game guide. That search is the item system doing exactly what it's supposed to — creating a live question that depends on what the enemy just built.

Counter-building is the specific skill this enables: switching item purchases in response to what the enemy team is constructing. Naavik's analysis of Brawl Stars notes that hero brawlers offer "less depth than progression-based or strategy games" specifically because they lack this layer. Its absence is what makes hero brawlers more accessible. Its presence in classic MOBAs is what creates the in-game strategic conversation that persists across the entire match — every gold increment is a decision about what to buy next given the current game state. When a new item release opens up a previously unexplored build path on an underperforming champion, that is the system working at its best: the item pool becomes a source of ongoing discovery rather than a static lookup.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Multiple viable build paths per champion that produce meaningfully different outcomes. Counter-building is impactful — buying the right item against specific threats noticeably changes match dynamics. Item interactions create strategic possibilities players discover rather than find in a guide. New item additions regularly unlock previously unexplored builds on existing champions. What you buy in game 10 against a fed enemy bruiser is a genuinely different answer than what you bought in game 1 against a control mage. | Your build is a decision, not a lookup. What you buy in response to the enemy feels like real strategy. |
| 4 | Good build variety with clear situational paths. Counter-building is meaningful. Most champions have 2-3 viable builds that perform differently. New items occasionally shift the meta. Some champion builds are over-solved but most retain meaningful decision points across different game states. | Build diversity is real. You adapt your purchases per game and it makes a difference. |
| 3 | Some build variety but an optimal path exists for most champions that experienced players follow by default. Counter-building is possible but deviation from the standard build rarely shifts outcomes enough to feel decisive. Item additions do not meaningfully expand the strategic space for most champions. | You follow the guide most of the time. Deviating is occasionally right but it's rarely clearly better. |
| 2 | One correct build per champion that the community has fully solved. Counter-building exists in theory but the correct play is almost always to complete core items first. Item variety is cosmetic rather than strategic. | You copy the pro build and follow it. Any deviation is usually a mistake rather than a decision. |
| 1 | No meaningful itemisation depth. Item selection is scripted or so simplified that no strategic decision is made during the match. The build layer does not function as a strategic system. | Items are power increases, not decisions. |
