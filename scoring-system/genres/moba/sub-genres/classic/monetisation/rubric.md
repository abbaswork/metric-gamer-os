# Metric: Monetisation Model
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Classic
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = fully cosmetic-only spending with a generous free experience, 1 = pay-to-win elements or a free experience so restricted it effectively requires payment to be competitive

## Why This Matters

Classic MOBAs are free-to-play without exception — the research is explicit: "if it wasn't free and had less playerbase I wouldn't play it." Players accept F2P on one condition: that nothing affecting match outcomes is locked behind payment. The line is hard — cosmetic-only monetisation is accepted as fair; anything affecting champion access in ways that create meaningful competitive disadvantage crosses into P2W territory.

In classic MOBAs, champion unlock costs are the primary F2P friction point. If a player cannot access a large enough champion pool to be competitive in draft-based modes — because too many champions are expensive and free earnings are slow — the free experience is deliberately handicapped. A player locked to 10 champions in a 5-ban draft environment is structurally disadvantaged compared to one with 60. Beyond champion access, skin pricing and cosmetic availability affect player goodwill even when they don't affect the fairness line — a game that prices skins at a rate players broadly find exploitative erodes trust in the developer even without pay-to-win. Dota 2 is commonly cited as the gold standard for cosmetic-only monetisation with no gameplay-gating; League of Legends and Mobile Legends sit at different points on the access speed spectrum.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Nothing affecting gameplay is behind a paywall. Champions are earnable through play at a pace where a new player has a competitively viable pool within a few weeks of regular sessions. The free progression track is clear and consistent. Cosmetics are available for purchase at prices the majority of the community considers fair, with a meaningful free cosmetic track alongside. | You never feel like spending is the path to winning. The free experience is complete. |
| 4 | Cosmetic-only spending with a slightly slower champion unlock pace. A new player needs a few extra weeks before they have a full competitive pool, but the path is clear and achievable without payment. Most cosmetics are reasonably priced. | Fairly free. The grind exists but isn't exploitative. Spending is for preference or cosmetics, not competitive access. |
| 3 | Cosmetic-only but unlock pace is slow enough that new players feel meaningfully behind. A full champion pool requires either significant time or moderate spending. Some cosmetics are priced high enough that the value proposition is unclear to most players. | The free path works but is noticeably slow. New players feel the gap between themselves and veterans in champion access, not just skill. |
| 2 | Unlock pace for free players is slow enough to create real friction. New players may feel effectively compelled to spend to have a competitive champion pool for draft modes. Battle pass seasonal content creates consistent FOMO pressure even when the content is cosmetic. | The free experience feels deliberately limited. Spending becomes the obvious path to not feeling behind. |
| 1 | Pay-to-win elements are present — purchasable items, stat boosts, or champions that provide a meaningful in-match advantage. Or the free champion pool is so small that draft-based competitive play is effectively inaccessible without payment. | The game punishes non-payers in-match. Spending isn't optional for competitive play. |
