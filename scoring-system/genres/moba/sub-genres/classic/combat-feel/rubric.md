# Metric: Combat Feel
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Classic
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = responsive, visually clear, satisfying moment-to-moment combat, 1 = sluggish or cluttered abilities that disconnect action from result

## Why This Matters

Classic MOBAs are top-down games, which means combat feel is delivered through animation quality, sound design, projectile clarity, and kill feedback rather than direct physical sensation. Riot's own design documentation describes the goal as "the ability to understand what's happening in League and respond to it" — a deceptively simple standard that requires every ability animation, VFX layer, and audio cue to be calibrated against the principle that "the amount of attention an ability grabs should match its level of importance."

When this works, a perfectly executed play communicates clearly: the animation sells it, the sound confirms it, the kill notification lands with appropriate weight. When it doesn't, technically correct plays feel hollow. The Clarity article from Riot notes that the whole system depends on "skins that feel great with abilities that are clear even during the most chaotic teamfights" — chaos is the stress test. A game can feel great in 1v1 situations and fall apart the moment five abilities fire simultaneously.

Team fights are where combat feel tends to degrade. Visual noise from stacked effects makes it difficult to track what just happened, which erodes both decision-making and the satisfaction of executing a good play.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Abilities fire with immediate, clear visual feedback. Projectiles are readable under team-fight conditions — you can track your own abilities and react to incoming threats even at high ability density. Kill confirmation is satisfying; the game communicates clearly when you land the decisive blow. Animation quality makes each champion's kit feel distinct and purposeful. In 5v5 team fights the screen remains legible enough to make decisions. | Every ability you land feels deliberate and earned. You can read what's happening even when everything fires at once. |
| 4 | Good responsiveness and visual clarity in most situations. Occasional animation clutter in dense team fights but the core feel is strong. Kill feedback is present and satisfying. A few champions have animations that are misleading about timing or range, but they are the exception. | Feels good to play. The occasional chaotic team fight loses readability but the core combat is responsive. |
| 3 | Adequate combat feel with some clunkiness. Abilities are functional but animations feel generic or dated. Kill feedback exists but doesn't carry the weight it should. Team fight clarity is inconsistent — you sometimes lose track of what's happening during the decisive moment. | Serviceable. The game plays correctly but doesn't make you feel impressive when you do well. |
| 2 | Sluggish or unresponsive ability activation. Visual noise obscures important combat information in team fights. Kill feedback is weak or absent. Several champion animations are misleading about hit ranges or timing, creating situations where play feels unfair rather than mistaken. | The game feels like it's fighting you. Clicks don't always feel connected to results. |
| 1 | Significant input delay or unresponsive abilities. Team fight readability collapses entirely. Kill and hit feedback are missing or wrong. The gap between what you click and what the game shows you is large enough to affect fundamental decisions. | Combat feels broken. You can't trust what the game is showing you. |
