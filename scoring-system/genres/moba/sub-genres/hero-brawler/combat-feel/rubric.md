# Metric: Combat Feel
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Hero Brawler
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = fast, responsive, visually satisfying combat that makes moment-to-moment play feel exciting, 1 = sluggish or hollow combat that makes individual plays feel unrewarding

## Why This Matters

Hero brawlers are more action-oriented than classic MOBAs. Combat is faster, matches are shorter, and individual encounters happen more frequently. This shifts combat feel from a strategic communication layer into a direct visceral experience: do abilities feel snappy and powerful? Does landing a knockout blow feel satisfying? Is the feedback of hitting versus missing clear and immediate?

Naavik notes that "short rounds and near-instant matchmaking keep players hooked" in Brawl Stars — the pacing that defines the sub-genre only works if the combat within each short round is consistently rewarding. Heroes of the Storm addresses this specifically through design: "Healing Fountains and Globes likewise help players stay in the fight longer, giving HotS matches more action than a typical MOBA" (Game Rant). The design deliberately increases the frequency of combat contact so every session feels more active than a classic MOBA's early laning phases. In all cases, the feel of the combat needs to justify the pace the sub-genre promises.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Abilities fire with immediate feedback and satisfying visual and audio response. Hitting versus missing is unambiguous. Knockouts and decisive plays are communicated with clear, rewarding animations. Combat pace feels consistently energetic without becoming visually chaotic — you can read what's happening in a 3v3 team fight. Special abilities and ultimates feel impactful every time they activate. | Landing a good ability or play feels genuinely exciting. The game communicates skill clearly through how combat feels. |
| 4 | Good combat responsiveness and feedback in most situations. Some visual noise in busy team fights but the core feel is strong. Hit confirmation is clear. A few abilities feel slightly underwhelming relative to their apparent role, but they are exceptions. | Feels good to play. Combat is engaging without being frustrating to read. |
| 3 | Adequate combat feel but inconsistently rewarding. Some abilities feel snappy while others feel sluggish or generic. Hit feedback is present but not particularly satisfying. Team fight readability is inconsistent — you sometimes lose track of what happened after a chaotic exchange. | The game plays correctly but does not make you feel impressive when you do well. Combat is functional, not exciting. |
| 2 | Sluggish ability responses or weak feedback that disconnects action from result. Visual noise makes busy fights difficult to read. Knockout animations or hit confirmation are weak enough that decisive moments do not feel earned. Individual play skill does not feel communicated by the game's visual language. | The game does not reward good play with good feel. Wins and losses feel similar moment-to-moment. |
| 1 | Severely unresponsive abilities, missing or incorrect hit feedback, or visual chaos that makes it impossible to track what's happening in combat. Combat does not deliver the action experience the sub-genre promises. | The combat feels broken. You can't trust the game to show you what's happening. |
