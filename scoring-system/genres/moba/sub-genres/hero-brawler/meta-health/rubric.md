# Metric: Meta Health
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Hero Brawler
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = broad brawler/hero viability across modes with a meta that evolves through patches, 1 = dominant tier that makes most of the roster feel pointless to play

## Why This Matters

Hero brawlers face a meta problem classic MOBAs don't: the meta isn't one-dimensional. A brawler that dominates Gem Grab might be weak in Showdown. This mode-specific complexity can mask broader imbalance — a brawler can look "balanced on average" while being oppressively strong in the highest-played modes. Zleague.gg documents what this looks like in the Brawl Stars community: players frustrated with brawlers like Eve dominating the current meta, while "suggestions for buffs to other less powerful brawlers highlight the desire for a more balanced competition." The community shows "a shared desire for innovation rather than just outright destruction of overpowered brawlers," wanting dynamic gameplay instead of a solved tier.

Tier list searches confirm players track meta state actively across all hero brawler titles: "brawl stars meta" at +30%, "pokemon unite meta" at +50%, "honor of kings meta" steady. The research also establishes the core motivation: "it constantly changes, metas, new champs, reworks etc. Other games don't and are stale to play." The failure mode is stagnation.

The absence of itemisation in hero brawlers makes a broken meta more painful than in classic MOBAs. You cannot counter-build against a broken brawler. You can only avoid their modes or out-rotate them in mode selection, which is a significantly blunter tool.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Wide brawler/hero viability across the primary modes. The S-tier shifts meaningfully with each patch. No single character dominates every mode — the best pick in one mode is not the best in another. Playing off-meta is viable at most skill levels and in most modes. Patches address imbalance at a pace that prevents any broken state from persisting more than a single update cycle. | Your character is not a liability in the modes you want to play. The tier list is a guide, not a constraint. |
| 4 | Healthy overall meta with occasional patches that create temporary imbalance. Most brawlers/heroes are viable in at least some modes. A clear S-tier exists but is mode-specific rather than universal. Balance updates arrive frequently enough that dominant states do not define extended play periods. | Meta is generally fair. Brief imbalances resolve without defining the experience. |
| 3 | A clear tier above the rest across multiple modes. Patches address issues but not always swiftly — a broken brawler might persist for 2-3 updates. Playing preferred characters in their weaker modes comes at a visible competitive cost. Higher-level play starts to feel constrained by the tier list. | The meta influences choices more than preference does at higher skill levels. Off-meta is viable but noticeably harder. |
| 2 | Narrow meta with a small set of brawlers/heroes dominating across most modes. Patches are slow or insufficient — dominant states persist for extended periods. A significant portion of the roster feels irrelevant to competitive play. Players feel compelled to use specific characters regardless of personal preference. | The meta dictates your character selection. Playing outside the dominant tier is a consistent disadvantage. |
| 1 | Solved meta where 3-5 brawlers/heroes dominate across all modes. The rest of the roster is effectively unplayable at competitive levels. Balance updates are absent, infrequent, or consistently fail to shift the dominant set. | If you're not playing the correct characters, you're losing to meta. The roster's variety is theoretical. |
