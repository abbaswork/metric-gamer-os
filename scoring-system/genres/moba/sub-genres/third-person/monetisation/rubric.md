# Metric: Monetisation Model
**Genre:** MOBA
**Sub-genre:** Third-Person
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = cosmetic-only spending with fair character access for free players, 1 = pay-to-win elements or a free character pool so restricted it disadvantages non-payers in competitive play

## Why This Matters

Third-person MOBAs occupy an unusual monetisation position within the genre. Smite's God Pack — a one-time purchase giving permanent access to all current and future gods — is widely cited as one of the most player-friendly unlock models in any live service MOBA. Deadlock entered early access without a finalised monetisation model. Predecessor operates on a different model again. The absence of a settled industry standard across this small sub-genre means monetisation quality varies more widely than in classic MOBAs, where the template is well-established.

The core fairness line is the same regardless of sub-genre: cosmetic-only spending is accepted; anything affecting match outcomes or creating meaningful competitive disadvantage from character access restrictions crosses it. In third-person MOBAs with smaller player bases, unfair monetisation has an amplified negative effect. A smaller community is less forgiving of models that feel exploitative, and negative word-of-mouth spreads through a proportionally larger fraction of the player base. The research captures the baseline expectation: "low barrier to entry: most MOBAs are F2P." Third-person MOBAs that deviate from fair F2P models face higher churn risk given their smaller pools.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Nothing affecting gameplay is behind a paywall. God/character access is either completely free, unlockable at a fair pace through play, or available via a one-time purchase that gives permanent access. Cosmetics are purchasable at fair prices with a meaningful free cosmetic track. Free players have a competitive character pool within the first few weeks of regular play. | The game earns money without making you feel it. The free experience is genuinely complete. |
| 4 | Cosmetic-only spending with a slightly slower free character unlock pace. A new player needs a few extra weeks to have a full competitive roster but the path is clear. A God Pack or similar one-time unlock represents reasonable value for players who want instant full access. Most cosmetics are reasonably priced. | Fairly free. The grind exists but it's not exploitative. A reasonable route to full access exists if you want to spend once rather than grind. |
| 3 | Cosmetic-only but unlock pace creates real friction or the one-time unlock price is high enough that it is not accessible to many players. Multiple currency systems make it difficult to understand the real cost of unlocks. Cosmetic pricing is frequently criticised by the community as too high for the player base size. | Free but slower than it should be. The community perception of fairness is mixed. |
| 2 | Unlock pace is slow enough to create meaningful competitive friction. The free character pool is limited enough to disadvantage new players in draft-based modes. Battle pass content or limited-time offers create consistent FOMO pressure. The community broadly considers the monetisation unfair. | The free experience feels limited. Spending feels required to participate fully in competitive modes. |
| 1 | Pay-to-win elements present, or the free character pool is so small that competitive ranked play is inaccessible without payment. In a sub-genre with a small player base, this level of monetisation aggression is particularly corrosive to the community. | Non-payers are disadvantaged in-match. The game cannot retain a healthy player base with this model. |
