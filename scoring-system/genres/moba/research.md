Why people love moba games:

- group game: friends will try to get other friends to play together with them, so a bit of virality going on there.

- easy watching and esports: extra visibility for MOBAs

Even if they play Action or Fighting games, it's pretty easy to draw many parallels to MOBAs.
- Generally it's a team sport with reasonable amounts of variation and depth. It also mixes team skill and personal skill reasonably well so if you are very good you will do well for the team but no one person will win a game singlehandedly except in the absolute worst mismatches.

- They're competitive team games that are fun when played casually. Thus the reasons that something like soccer/football is popular mostly apply to MOBAs as well. Relatively low barrier to entry (F2P, easy to acquire, not too high system requirements, easy to understand overall goal but a lot of complexity in getting there), fun nearly immediately (esp. if you enjoy learning/figuring things out), but have a lot of depth if you want to sink more time into them.
- Map interaction: FMobas tend to design maps in ways that allow classes and champions to have interesting interaction with the environment.
- Strategy Tactics Blend: Most PvP games tend to showcase either strong tactical play (Team Fortress 2, for example), or strong strategical play (Starcraft 2, for example), without being able to encourage both styles simultaneously.
- MOBAs give the player a strong sense of personal success when they do well... but a low sense of personal responsibility when they do poorly.
- they're fun and complex with generous amounts of strategy and skill.
