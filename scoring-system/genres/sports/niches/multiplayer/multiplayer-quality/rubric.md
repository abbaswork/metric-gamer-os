# Metric: Multiplayer Quality
**Genre:** Sports
**Sub-genre:** Football (niche: Multiplayer)
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = stable netcode and fair matchmaking, 1 = lag and input delay regularly decide matches

## Why This Matters

This niche metric swaps in for Presentation & Atmosphere when scoring a multiplayer-focused page — online head-to-head play (Ultimate-Team-style or ranked seasons) or Football Manager's online leagues. Presentation matters less when the actual concern shifts to whether the online experience itself is fair and stable.

Online input delay and connection-quality complaints are among the longest-running, most consistently documented community grievances in the FIFA/FC Ultimate Team online community — players have discussed "input delay" and connection-dependent advantage for years, often noting that a technically worse player with a better connection can beat a better player on a worse one, which undermines the skill expression the on-pitch metrics are supposed to reward. Matchmaking fairness (being paired against opponents far outside your actual skill or squad-value bracket) is a closely related, similarly well-documented complaint.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Online matches feel as responsive as offline play in the vast majority of games; matchmaking pairs reasonably similar skill/squad levels; connection issues are rare and clearly flagged when they happen. | Online feels like the same game you play offline. Who wins is about who played better. |
| 4 | Generally stable and fair, but a noticeable minority of matches have input delay or mismatched opponents. | Mostly solid. You'll hit the occasional bad connection or lopsided match. |
| 3 | Inconsistent — input delay and mismatched matchmaking happen often enough to be a regular topic of frustration, without making the mode unplayable. | You'll win and lose matches you shouldn't have, and you'll usually know why. |
| 2 | Input delay or unfair matchmaking happens frequently enough to regularly decide outcomes independent of skill. | Plenty of losses (and wins) aren't really about how you played. |
| 1 | Online play is regularly unplayable or decided by connection quality rather than skill — input delay, disconnects, or wildly mismatched opponents are the norm. | Online mode is something to avoid, not a selling point. |
