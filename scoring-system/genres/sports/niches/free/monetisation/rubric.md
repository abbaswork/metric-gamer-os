# Metric: Monetisation
**Genre:** Sports
**Sub-genre:** Football (niche: Free)
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = free-to-play progression is achievable and fair without paying, 1 = competitive viability is effectively locked behind spending

## Why This Matters

This niche metric swaps in for Content & Game Modes (action sims) or Transfer Market & Squad Building (free management sims like Top Eleven) when scoring a free-to-play page. eFootball and FC Ultimate Team-centric content both monetise primarily through randomised player/pack acquisition — a model that has drawn some of the most sustained regulatory and community scrutiny of any monetisation system in gaming, including loot-box-specific regulatory action in multiple countries targeting exactly this kind of randomised, real-money player-pack mechanic.

The complaint isn't that monetisation exists — it's whether competitive viability is realistically achievable through free play, or whether the gap between a paying and non-paying squad becomes wide enough that skill stops being the deciding factor. That's the same underlying concern as Competitive Balance, but applied specifically to the free-to-play economy rather than match-engine scripting.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | A competitive squad is realistically achievable through free play within a reasonable timeframe; paid options accelerate progress but don't unlock anything free play genuinely can't reach. | You can compete with players who spent money — it just takes you longer to get there. |
| 4 | Free progression is viable but slower than intended, or a specific tier of content (a particular event or top-end player pool) is meaningfully harder to reach without spending. | You can get most of the way there for free. The very top is a real grind or a real cost. |
| 3 | Free play gets you a functional squad, but the gap to paying players is large enough to be a regular disadvantage in competitive modes. | You can play. You'll notice who paid and who didn't. |
| 2 | Meaningful competitive progress requires spending in practice, even if it's technically possible for free — the free path is disproportionately slow or randomised. | The game is "free," but competing seriously isn't, really. |
| 1 | Competitive viability is effectively locked behind spending — free-to-play progression cannot realistically reach a competitive squad. | This is a paid game wearing a free price tag. |
