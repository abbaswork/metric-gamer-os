# Metric: Transfer Market & Squad Building
**Genre:** Sports
**Sub-genre:** Football
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = scouting and negotiation are deep, information-driven systems with real risk and reward, 1 = transfers are a flat menu with no negotiation, scouting, or uncertainty

## Why This Matters

This metric is specific to management sims — there is no on-pitch equivalent, which is exactly why it sits in the pool alongside On-Pitch Match Feel rather than competing with it for the same slot. Football Manager's scouting and negotiation system is the single most structurally unique mechanic separating it from every action sim in this category: assigning scouts, interpreting incomplete information about a player's ability, negotiating fees and wages against a club's resistance, and managing a transfer budget across a season are mechanics no action sim attempts at all.

research.md's "Developing Their Teams and Players" Reddit factor touches the adjacent progression side of squad-building, but transfer market depth is its own distinct skill — finding and acquiring talent rather than developing what you already have — and it's one of the most consistently cited reasons management-sim players stay invested season over season.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Scouting surfaces genuinely incomplete or uncertain information that rewards investment in the right scouts and regions; negotiations involve real friction (fee resistance, wage demands, agent fees, release clauses) with outcomes that vary based on your approach, not just your budget size. | Finding your next star feels like detective work, and getting the deal done feels like a real negotiation, not a purchase. |
| 4 | Scouting and negotiation have real depth and uncertainty, but one part of the loop is thinner than the rest — maybe negotiation is mostly budget-gated with little room for tactics, or scouting accuracy doesn't vary meaningfully by scout quality. | The system has teeth, but a specific part of it boils down to "do you have enough money." |
| 3 | Scouting and negotiation exist as discrete steps, but information is mostly complete upfront and negotiation outcomes are largely predictable from budget alone. | You go through the motions of scouting and negotiating, but the outcome was rarely in doubt. |
| 2 | Transfers involve minimal scouting (stats are visible immediately) and negotiation is a single take-it-or-leave-it offer. | It's a shopping list with a price tag, not a market. |
| 1 | No scouting uncertainty and no negotiation — players are bought directly off a flat list with fixed prices. | There's no market here at all, just a catalogue. |
