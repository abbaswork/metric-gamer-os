# Metric: License Authenticity
**Genre:** Sports
**Sub-genre:** Football
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = real clubs, players, kits, and competitions throughout, 1 = generic fictional names and kits across most of the game

## Why This Matters

"Clubs and Players they support or enjoy watching" is a direct Reddit factor in research.md, and the Keywords Findings note that a large share of football-game search volume is built around real-world branding ("Fifa", specific clubs, "World Cup") rather than generic genre terms — players search for the leagues and stars they follow, not "football game" in the abstract.

This metric is also the single most direct line to two of the five games in the current PC roster: Football Life 25 and Smokepatch exist *specifically* because Konami's eFootball (the 2021 rebrand of Pro Evolution Soccer) operates with partial licensing — some leagues and clubs are fully licensed, others ship with placeholder names, badges, and kits. Both patches' entire purpose is restoring the real-world identity the base game is missing. That's not a niche modding curiosity — it's direct, well-documented evidence of how much this metric matters to the audience: enough that a dedicated community maintains unofficial patches purely to fix it, on a yearly cycle, for free.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Real clubs, players, kits, stadiums, and competitions across the leagues and competitions players actually follow — including major international tournaments where relevant. Likenesses are current and accurately updated. | The teams, players, and competitions you actually care about are all there, looking right. |
| 4 | Strong licensing coverage across major leagues and clubs, but specific gaps exist — a notable league, a handful of clubs, or one competition missing real branding. | The big names are covered. A few specific teams or competitions you might care about aren't. |
| 3 | Mixed licensing — roughly half of relevant clubs/leagues are real, the rest use placeholder names, kits, or badges that require a separate patch or in-game editor to fix. | You'll recognise plenty of teams. You'll also hit "Newcastle United" reskinned as something generic more than once. |
| 2 | Licensing is sparse — most clubs and competitions outside a handful of flagship leagues use generic names and kits. | Outside the biggest leagues, almost nothing is recognisable without outside fixes. |
| 1 | Little to no real-world licensing — names, kits, and badges are fictional across nearly the entire game. | This could be any football game. None of it is *your* team. |
