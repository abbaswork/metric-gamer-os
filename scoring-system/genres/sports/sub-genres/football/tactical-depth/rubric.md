# Metric: Tactical Depth & AI
**Genre:** Sports
**Sub-genre:** Football
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = tactical choices visibly and consistently change match outcomes, 1 = tactics are cosmetic and the AI plays the same regardless

## Why This Matters

"Trying Different Strategies and Seeing if They Work or Not" is one of research.md's clearest Reddit factors, and it's unusual in the pool for applying almost identically across both contexts this genre spans — setting formations and instructions mid-match in an action sim is the same underlying impulse as building a tactical philosophy across a season in a management sim, just expressed at different time scales.

Football Manager's entire reputation is built on this single axis more than any other game in the category — its role-and-duty tactical system (assigning each player a position, role, and duty that interacts with team shape and instructions) is the most-discussed mechanic in its community, to the point that "tactics" forums and tactic-sharing are a genre unto themselves within the FM fanbase. On the action-sim side, EA Sports FC and PES/eFootball both let players adjust formations, marking schemes, and player instructions mid-match — the metric scores whether changing them actually changes what happens on the pitch, or whether the AI plays the same way regardless of what's been set.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Tactical changes — formation, player roles/duties, in-match instructions — produce clear, attributable shifts in how matches play out. The same squad can win or lose based on tactical setup alone, and the game gives you enough feedback to understand why. | Two managers with the same players can have completely different seasons. Your tactical choices are the story. |
| 4 | Tactical systems have real depth and visibly affect outcomes, but feedback on *why* something worked or failed is thin, or a subset of tactical options (e.g. very specific role combinations) don't meaningfully differ from defaults. | Tactics matter, but you're sometimes guessing at why a change worked. |
| 3 | Basic tactical options exist (formation, a few instructions) and produce some effect, but the difference between a "good" and "bad" tactical setup is small relative to raw player quality. | Tactics are a minor lever, not a defining one — talent does most of the work. |
| 2 | Tactical options exist on paper but rarely change AI or match behaviour in any consistent, learnable way. | You can fiddle with settings, but you can't tell if they're doing anything. |
| 1 | Tactics are cosmetic — formation and instructions have no detectable effect on how the AI or simulation plays. | Why bother — the match plays itself regardless of what you set. |
