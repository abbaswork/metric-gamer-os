# Metric: Weapon Access
**Sub-genre:** Shooters — Free
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = competitive weapons fully earnable, 1 = best weapons locked behind payment

## Why This Matters

Weapon access is the shooter-specific pay-to-win concern that sits beneath the broader monetisation model question. In a game where gunfights are the primary mechanic, locking competitively superior weapons behind a paywall is a more direct form of pay-to-win than in most other genres — you can see the weapon stats, look up the tier lists, and find out precisely what the performance gap is between what you can earn and what others paid for.

The specific failure mode the community documents is twofold. The first is the weapon blueprint problem: paid weapon blueprints in games like Call of Duty include unique iron sights or reticles that provide an aiming advantage over the default version of the same weapon — technically the same gun, functionally different in a heads-up engagement. The second is the locked-weapon problem: newer, stronger weapons are introduced into the game through battle passes or store bundles and are not made earnable through gameplay, creating a growing power gap over time. Warzone is the documented case for both. By contrast, CS:GO and VALORANT's approach — all competitive weapons available to all players, paid content limited to weapon skins that are purely cosmetic — is the benchmark the competitive shooter community uses.

## Sources

- **r/CODWarzone** (training data) — paid weapon blueprint iron sight advantages are one of the most documented and controversial elements of Warzone's monetisation; the "no iron sights" advantage on paid blueprints is explicitly named in community discussions
- **r/GlobalOffensive** (training data) — CS:GO's "all weapons free, skins cosmetic" model is consistently cited as the fair weapon access standard; the community uses it as the reference when criticising other games
- **r/VALORANT** (training data) — weapon skin system is described as purely cosmetic by the community; all agents (characters) being earnable through play alongside free weapon access is cited positively
- **r/FreeGamesOnSteam and r/FreeGamesOnPSN** (training data) — "pay-to-win weapons" is a documented reason players leave a free shooter; "all weapons unlockable by playing" is a documented positive signal in community recommendations

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | All weapons with competitive relevance are earnable through gameplay at a rate proportional to time investment. Paid weapon blueprints provide cosmetic changes only — no iron sight advantages, no unique attachments unavailable on the free version. No new weapons are introduced exclusively through purchase. | The weapon you can earn and the weapon someone paid for perform the same when used. What costs money is how it looks. |
| 4 | Almost all competitive weapons are earnable through play. Some blueprints provide minor visual differences that could be interpreted as marginal advantages but are not meaningfully impactful in normal play. New weapons are introduced through earned and paid tiers simultaneously. | You can build a competitive loadout without spending. Some paid blueprints have minor aesthetic advantages, but nothing you'll notice or miss in a match. |
| 3 | Most competitive weapons are earnable but some notable exceptions exist. Certain blueprints provide acknowledged advantages in specific situations. New weapons may be time-gated to the paid battle pass before becoming earnable. | You can compete for free. You'll encounter some weapons you can't access yet, and a few of them will feel unfair in specific situations. |
| 2 | Several competitively relevant weapons are locked to paid content for meaningful periods. Some paid blueprints provide aiming or attachment advantages. The free weapon pool becomes outdated as new weapons are introduced exclusively through purchase. | Your loadout is behind the meta. You're competing with last season's weapons against players using this season's, and some of the difference is real. |
| 1 | The competitive meta is defined by paywalled weapons. Paid blueprints include iron sight or attachment advantages that affect engagement outcomes. Free players cannot replicate the top-tier loadout regardless of time invested. | Weapon access is the game's pay-to-win mechanic. The combat is fair in theory and unfair in practice because the best tools cost money. |
