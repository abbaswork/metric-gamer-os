# Metric: Monetisation Model
**Sub-genre:** Shooters — Free
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = monetisation problems absent, 1 = the business model defines the experience

## Why This Matters

Free-to-play shooters have a specific monetisation challenge that other free genres don't share: the battle pass. Because shooters are the genre where battle passes became the dominant model, they are also the genre with the most documented community debate about when a battle pass is fair and when it becomes a pay-to-progress system.

The community distinction the FPS community makes is between cosmetic and gameplay content in the same battle pass. A battle pass that locks weapon blueprint skins, sprays, and operator skins behind its paid tier is accepted — these are cosmetic. A battle pass where the paid tier unlocks XP bonuses, exclusive operators with different hitboxes, or weapon blueprints with different iron sights than the free base variant crosses into gameplay impact. Fortnite's original battle pass is the positive reference: cosmetic-only, battle pass progression independent of match performance. Warzone's operator skin hitbox issue — paid operator skins with smaller or differently-shaped hitboxes than default — is the documented failure case. Apex Legends' Legend unlock system is a separate debate: Legends are playable characters with unique abilities, and the question of whether they should be earnable is a consistent community discussion.

## Sources

- **r/FortNiteBR** (training data) — Fortnite battle pass cosmetic-only model is cited as the acceptable benchmark; operator skin hitbox advantages are not present in Fortnite and the community notes this positively
- **r/CODWarzone** (training data) — operator skin hitbox controversy is one of the most documented pay-to-win issues in the game's history; paid skins with smaller hitboxes are named specifically
- **r/ApexLegends** (training data) — Legend unlock rate and whether new Legends should be free is a recurring community debate; the competitive argument is that each Legend has a unique kit that affects match outcomes
- **Steam reviews (free shooters)** (training data) — "pay-to-win battle pass" is a documented negative tag; "cosmetic only, fair game" is a documented positive signal

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | All gameplay-affecting content — weapons, abilities, characters, operators — is accessible through play. The battle pass, if one exists, is cosmetic only. No paid tier provides a hitbox, movement, or mechanical advantage. XP progression is not locked behind payment. | The free experience is the full competitive game. What you look like costs money. What you shoot with and who you play as doesn't. |
| 4 | Almost all gameplay content is accessible for free. Some content takes longer to earn without spending, but nothing behind the paid tier provides a meaningful competitive advantage. Battle pass rewards are primarily cosmetic with minor non-gameplay additions. | You'll access the competitive meta through play. The battle pass might have things you want, but you're not missing gameplay because you didn't buy it. |
| 3 | Some gameplay-affecting content — an operator ability, a weapon blueprint with different iron sights, or an XP bonus that affects unlock speed — sits behind the paid tier. The core game is competitive for free players but the paid tier provides acknowledged advantages. | You can compete for free. You'll notice what the paid tier does to the competitive environment, and some of it will affect matches. |
| 2 | The paid battle pass includes content that meaningfully affects competitive outcomes. Operator hitboxes, weapon unlock speed gates, or character ability access create a two-tier competitive environment. Free players are at a structural disadvantage in ranked or competitive modes. | Paying buys competitive advantage, not just cosmetics. The game is not free-to-win, but it isn't free-to-compete-equally either. |
| 1 | Paid content provides direct, measurable competitive advantages. Operator hitboxes are smaller, weapons are quantifiably stronger in the paid tier, or abilities are gatekept behind purchase. The free experience is a lobby for paying players to farm. | The game has pay-to-win mechanics. The competitive environment is defined by who spent money, not who got better. |
