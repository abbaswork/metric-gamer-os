# Metric: Weapon Variety & Customization
**Genre:** Shooters
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = diverse arsenal where each weapon serves a distinct purpose and customization meaningfully changes how it handles, 1 = one dominant option that makes the rest irrelevant, with customization that's cosmetic only

## Why This Matters

A wide arsenal only matters if the weapons in it serve different roles. The community standard is precise: "no single useless weapon." Each weapon class should solve a specific problem that others can't solve as efficiently. When this works, players make active tactical decisions about what to carry. When it fails, the variety exists on paper and nowhere else.

Doom Eternal is the community reference for this done right. Every weapon has a specific use case in every major encounter. The shotgun is not interchangeable with the plasma rifle, and the game designs encounters around that. On the other end, games where one assault rifle class dominates across all ranges and modes reduce the rest of the arsenal to unlockables.

Customization is the second half of this metric, not a separate concern. Attachments, mods, and build options are what let a player turn a base weapon into something suited to their own playstyle — a scope that trades fire rate for range, a barrel that trades handling for damage. Research into Battlefield and Call of Duty communities flags "weapon variety + customization" as one combined factor players raise together, not two separate ones: a wide arsenal with shallow or purely cosmetic attachments still reads as thin, because the variety that matters is the variety a player can build into a weapon they already like.

## Sources

- **gamedesignskills.com: "FPS Game Design"** — "there should be no single useless weapon in the game"; "a wide arsenal of powerful weapons should serve distinct purposes while all feeling equally weighty" (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **gamerant.com: "Shooters With The Best Gunplay"** — Doom Eternal cited specifically for arsenal design where players "pick up everything from plasma rifles to beefy revolvers with every single one feeling rewarding" and where "different playstyles work just as well" (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/FPS and r/Doom** (training data) — weapon utility debates are common in shooter communities; games where one weapon dominates are regularly cited as balance failures; Doom Eternal's weapon wheel is frequently held as the community standard for meaningful variety
- **r/GlobalOffensive and r/Valorant** (training data) — loadout discussions and weapon tier lists show that players engage deeply with weapon variety when the distinctions are real
- **research/shooters/readme.md** — user-curated research from Reddit discussions of Battlefield 5/1/2042 and Call of Duty Black Ops 2/Modern Warfare 2; "Weapon variety + Customization" listed as a single combined recurring factor

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Each weapon class has a defined role that another class can't fully replace, and attachments or mods meaningfully change handling — trade-offs, not just stat bumps. The choice of what to carry and how to build it changes how you approach encounters. | You'll swap weapons and rebuild loadouts because the situation calls for it. The arsenal keeps asking you to make decisions on two levels. |
| 4 | Most weapons have clear use cases with some overlap between adjacent classes. Customization options create real trade-offs for the weapons players actually use, even if a few attachments are clearly better with no downside. | You'll settle on a loadout but tune it meaningfully, and other weapons still feel like real options. |
| 3 | A broad selection of weapons that cover the same roles with surface-level differences. Attachments exist but mostly add flat stat improvements rather than trade-offs, so there's usually one "correct" build per weapon. | You'll find your go-to quickly, attach the obvious upgrades, and stop thinking about it. |
| 2 | The arsenal is thin or homogeneous. Most weapons operate the same way with minor stat differences, and attachments are mostly cosmetic or provide negligible changes to handling. | The loadout screen looks deep. In practice you're using the same two things, built the same way, every session. |
| 1 | One weapon or weapon class dominates every scenario, and customization is cosmetic only with no effect on handling or performance. | You find the best option early, skin it, and put the rest down. |
