### Reddit Important Factors — r/fps intro post, 2026-07-06 (OP's own 5/5 definitions — source of truth for checklists)
The original poster's reply to their own thread defined what a 5/5 means for six metrics in their own words, in response to community feedback on their intro post. These quotes are the primary source for each metric's checklist — the rubric exists to support these definitions, not the other way around.
- **Gunplay:** "Every weapon has its own 'feel' and attachments that create real strategy and trade-offs rather than flat stat bumps." Example given: the MW2 (2022) ninja loadout — heartbeat sensor + smoke screen — as a build that changes your whole approach to a fight, not just its stats.
- **Game Modes:** "The game modes are actually active, I can run them both online and offline, they flow really well based on the maps and compliment the perk/squad system in the game and they are genuinely memorable. I'm talking BO2 Sticks and Stones levels." Note: "online and offline" (bot/local play support) is a dimension not previously in this metric at all.
- **Movement:** "Its hard to explain this one but every movement feels satisfying, whether its the Black Ops running on walls or MW immersive type, it not only fits in with the other themes in the game but enhances it." Frames movement primarily as felt satisfaction and cross-system synergy, not just tactical utility.
- **Online Maps:** "I love maps with different positioning and flanking strategies instead of just one hotspot - Also maps that don't feel empty with a full lobby is pretty big one." The second half (map scale calibrated to lobby size) is a dimension not previously in this metric at all.
- **Online Play / Squads:** "Roles and classes actually matter - doesn't have to be strictly enforced but we can run squad play with different roles that contribute to the win - especially in tourney and hardcore matches." Adds that role-adherence is a soft expectation, most load-bearing in hardcore/competitive play specifically.
- **Campaign:** "The missions are fresh and introduce something new each time. I don't want my campaign to just feel like a reskin again and again. I don't mind short campaigns if they make me want to replay them and leave an impression. The gold standard here is the original MW2 stealth missions for me." Adds that campaign length doesn't matter if the missions are memorable and replayable.
