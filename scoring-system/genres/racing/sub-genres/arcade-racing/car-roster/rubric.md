# Metric: Car Roster
**Genre:** Racing
**Sub-genre:** Arcade Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = a large, varied roster of desirable vehicles spanning multiple classes, mostly accessible through normal play, 1 = a thin roster with few meaningful choices

## Why This Matters

research.md's Reddit list names "Cool cars" and "Variety of vehicles" as separate factors — and the search data confirms that roster is a purchase signal in its own right. "Forza Horizon 6 car list" appears as a standalone Google Trends query, and the keyword findings note that a large share of searches ask about "the best car" or "the location of X" — players are researching specific vehicles before, or instead of, just searching for the game. That means a roster isn't a background feature; it's something players evaluate independently when deciding whether to buy.

The Forza Horizon series is the genre reference point for why roster scale matters. Forza Horizon 5 launched with 902 licensed vehicles across road cars, off-roaders, hypercars, and classics, and the livery editor's Creator's Hub lets players share community designs — which turns a large count into a roster worth exploring rather than a spreadsheet. Scale alone isn't enough, though: Asphalt 8's car-pack monetisation structure has been criticised for "aggressive gating" that locks most of its nominally large roster behind grind or real-money purchases, which is functionally a small roster for anyone not spending. The key distinction is between a roster that's big on paper and one that's actually accessible.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Roster numbers in the hundreds, spanning multiple vehicle classes (street, muscle, hypercar, off-road, classic, etc.) and manufacturers, with the majority unlockable through normal play. | You can lose hours just browsing the garage, and the car you want is probably in there and earnable without paying extra. |
| 4 | A large and varied roster with good class coverage, but a meaningful slice of vehicles sits behind a paywall, steep grind, or timed event that most players won't clear. Alternatively, the count is high but variety is shallow — lots of cars, mostly from the same era or class. | There's a lot to choose from, but the roster has a visible ceiling you'll hit once you've played a while. |
| 3 | A reasonable selection with the expected classes represented — enough that you can find something for each race type — but limited depth in any one class and no standout "I have to drive that" moments. Counts in the dozens rather than hundreds. | The roster covers the bases without being a selling point on its own. |
| 2 | Roster is thin or heavily duplicated — cars within a tier feel like reskins rather than distinct vehicles — or the game advertises a large count but most vehicles are locked behind aggressive monetisation the majority of players won't clear. | The car list looks bigger than it plays. |
| 1 | A handful of vehicles total with no meaningful variety between classes or manufacturers, or a nominally present roster that's almost entirely inaccessible without real-money spending. | You race the same two or three cars from start to finish. |
