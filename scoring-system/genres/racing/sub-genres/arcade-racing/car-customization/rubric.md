# Metric: Car Roster & Customization
**Genre:** Racing
**Sub-genre:** Arcade Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = a wide vehicle roster with deep visual and performance customization that's actually attainable through normal play, 1 = a thin roster with customization that's largely cosmetic or locked behind grind and paywalls

## Why This Matters

research.md's Reddit list names "customization," "Cool cars," and "Variety of vehicles" as three separate factors players use to judge arcade racing games — more dedicated mentions than any other theme in the list except the driving-feel cluster (see [Driving Feel & Mastery](../driving-feel/rubric.md)). The Google Trends data backs this from the search side: "Forza Horizon 6 car list" appears as a named query in its own right, and the keyword findings note a large share of queries asking about "the best car" — players are actively researching the roster before, or instead of, just searching for the game itself.

Need for Speed: Underground is the genre's reference point for why this matters beyond just having options. The game rebuilt its entire car list around tuner culture and let players change everything from performance parts to vinyls, underglow, body kits, and custom bumpers — and the customization wasn't just popular, it reportedly turned players into real-life car modifiers and helped the game sell over 15 million copies. That's the bar: customization deep enough that it becomes a reason to own the game on its own, independent of the racing.

Roster size matters too, but scale isn't the whole story. Forza Horizon's car lists (618 to over 900 vehicles depending on the entry) come paired with a livery editor capable of painting individual body components and sharing community designs through an in-game marketplace, which is what turns a large count into a roster worth exploring rather than a spreadsheet. The failure mode the community calls out is when a nominally large roster is mostly inaccessible: Asphalt 8's car-pack monetisation structure has been criticised for "aggressive gating" that locks most of the roster behind grind or real-money purchases, which is functionally a small roster for anyone not paying.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Roster numbers in the hundreds and spans multiple vehicle classes, with the majority unlockable through normal play rather than gated behind real-money purchases. Customisation covers both performance (engine, handling, drivetrain) and deep visual options (body kits, liveries/vinyls, underglow, custom parts), with tools expressive enough that players build and share genuinely personal builds. | You'll find a car and a look that feels like yours, and you didn't need to pay extra to get there. |
| 4 | A large, varied roster with strong visual and performance customisation, but a meaningful slice of cars or cosmetic options sit behind a paywall or steep grind, or the customisation depth is strong on one axis (visual) and shallower on the other (performance). | There's plenty to work with, but the best stuff costs more time or money than it probably should. |
| 3 | A reasonable roster with the expected classes represented, and customisation that covers the basics (colour, a handful of part swaps) without going deep on either visual expression or performance tuning. | You can make the car yours in a basic way, but you won't spend hours in a livery editor. |
| 2 | Roster is thin or heavily duplicated across tiers, and customisation is mostly cosmetic palette-swaps with little performance impact, or most of the roster and customisation options are locked behind aggressive monetisation that most players won't clear through normal play. | The car list looks bigger than it plays — most of it is out of reach without paying. |
| 1 | A handful of vehicles with no meaningful customisation beyond a default skin, or a roster that's nominally large but almost entirely inaccessible without spending real money. | What you're given at the start is close to what you're stuck with. |
