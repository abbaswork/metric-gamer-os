# Metric: Character Roster & Customization
**Genre:** Racing
**Sub-genre:** Kart Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = a large roster of genuinely distinct, recognizable characters paired with deep, attainable visual and performance customization, 1 = a thin or heavily duplicated roster with customization that's cosmetic-only or barely present

## Why This Matters

research.md's Reddit list names "distinctive character personalities," "recognizable characters," and "in depth customization" as three separate factors — more individual mentions than any other theme except item balance — which fits how heavily kart racing leans on character identity as a selling point distinct from the karts themselves. This is also where the sub-genre most clearly diverges from arcade racing's [Car Roster & Customization](../../arcade-racing/car-customization/rubric.md): arcade racing's roster question is about the cars; kart racing's research puts the character first and treats kart/vehicle customization as the second layer on top of it.

Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds — directly named in research.md's Top Games list — is the current high-water mark: 23 characters spanning Team Sonic, the Babylon Rogues, and Team Chaotix alongside crossover guests from entirely outside the Sonic universe (Persona's Joker, Like a Dragon's Ichiban, Hatsune Miku), paired with over 45 original vehicles, 70-plus gadgets, and five vehicle classes that can be further tuned by swapping front, rear, and tire parts — customization deep enough that reviewers noted it "offered enough depth to reward experimentation" even next to Mario Kart World's simplicity. Hot Wheels Unleashed, also in research.md's Top Games list, makes the customization itself shareable: its Livery Editor lets players paint individual cars and publish the results for the community to browse and download.

Mario Kart 8 — research.md's own top-ranked entry — is also the genre's cautionary case for roster depth specifically, which shows this metric doesn't just reward the biggest name in the category by default. Community retrospectives single out the base roster for being "heavily weighted toward Koopalings and baby characters," with seven nearly-identical Koopalings criticized for taking up "almost a whole row of the roster" and characters like Pink Gold Peach singled out as "a complete waste of a character slot" that's "not unique at all." A roster that's numerically large but padded with near-duplicates reads thinner than the count suggests.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | A large roster where the large majority of characters are visually and mechanically distinct, not palette-swapped duplicates, paired with deep customization across both vehicle performance and visual identity — with the best options attainable through normal play rather than gated behind a paywall. | You'll find a character and a build that feels genuinely yours, not a recolor of someone else's. |
| 4 | A large, varied roster and strong customization options, but a meaningful slice of the roster is near-duplicate characters (palette swaps, baby/clone variants), or customization is deep on one axis (visual or performance) while shallow on the other. | There's a lot here, but a chunk of the roster is the same character wearing a different hat. |
| 3 | A reasonable roster with a handful of standout characters, and customization that covers the basics (a costume or two, simple part swaps) without much depth on either visual expression or performance tuning. | You'll find someone you like driving, but you won't spend hours building them out. |
| 2 | Roster is thin or padded with near-identical variants, and customization is mostly cosmetic palette-swaps with negligible performance impact. | The character select screen looks bigger than it plays. |
| 1 | A handful of characters with no meaningful distinction between them, and no customization beyond a default skin. | Pick whoever you want — it won't change how the race feels. |
