# Metric: Kart Customization & Creation
**Genre:** Racing
**Sub-genre:** Kart Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = kart parts meaningfully change performance AND appearance, players can build and share their own tracks, and all of it is reachable through normal play; 1 = one kart per character with no parts, no creation tools, and nothing to unlock

## Why This Matters

research.md's Reddit list names "in depth customization" as a standalone factor players use to judge kart racing games — and two of the four top-ranked games in the same document are defined primarily by their creation toolsets. ModNation Racers positioned itself explicitly around Sony's "Play, Create, Share" philosophy: its Creation Station let players build characters with over 200 customization options, assemble karts from scratch, and sculpt full tracks using a steamroller-driving editor that manipulates elevation, terrain, foliage, and item placement in real time. Reviewers called the track tools "extremely deep and flexible yet always user-friendly." Hot Wheels Unleashed's track builder was described by Game Developer as a direct extension of the same editor Milestone used to make the shipped tracks — meaning players weren't using a simplified toy version, they were using the production tool.

The performance side of the equation is just as important as the visual side, and the two are easy to confuse. Mario Kart 8 Deluxe's body/tires/glider system looks like a cosmetic chooser but hides a real stat layer underneath: each part contributes to Speed, Acceleration, Weight, Handling, Traction, and hidden sub-stats including Anti-Gravity Speed and Mini-Turbo charging rate — the last of which is the competitive meta, since small tires like the Roller maximize Mini-Turbo breakpoints and are the default choice for competitive players. The customization has teeth even if the menu doesn't fully explain it. The failure mode is the opposite: ModNation Racers' own critics noted that its kart customization is "solely aesthetic and provides no gameplay enhancements," which means a beautifully decorated kart and a default one perform identically — the visual depth doesn't translate into a reason to optimize.

The accessibility of customization content matters as much as its depth. Mario Kart Tour's gacha Pipe system — where characters, karts, and gliders were locked inside randomized pulls costing up to $236.96 for a full set — drew a lawsuit describing it as "immoral," and Nintendo eventually removed the mechanic entirely. KartRider: Drift went the opposite direction: purely cosmetic customization with no performance stat variation, framed as a fair-play decision. Neither is the ideal; the top of this scale requires both meaningful content and clean access to it through normal play.

## Rubric

Scores are assessed across three axes:
- **Performance customization** — do parts or vehicle choices meaningfully change how the kart handles?
- **Visual customization** — can players make their kart and racer look distinct and personal?
- **Creation & sharing** — can players build and share their own tracks or content?

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Performance:** Kart parts (body, tires, and equivalent slots) produce real, meaningful stat differences — competitive players optimize combinations and the choices visibly affect race outcomes. **Visual:** Players can customize the appearance of their kart and racer independently of performance, with enough options that no two garages look identical. **Creation:** A track or content editor lets players build and share their own races online, using the same tools or close equivalents to what the developers used. All content is unlockable through normal play. | You can spend as long building and tuning as you do racing — and what you make is yours to share. |
| 4 | Strong on two of the three axes but weak on one — for example, real performance differentiation and visual depth but no creation tools, or deep creation and visual tools where all karts perform identically, or strong across all three but meaningful content locked behind DLC or steep grind. | The customization is genuinely deep, but you'll hit a clear ceiling on whichever dimension is missing or gated. |
| 3 | Performance differences between vehicle choices exist and are noticeable (different classes handle differently), some visual options are present, but there are no creation or sharing tools, and the customization depth on both axes is limited enough that it functions as a feature rather than a focus. | You can build a kart that feels different to drive without spending much time on it — and that's roughly as deep as it goes. |
| 2 | Customization is purely cosmetic — all karts and characters perform identically regardless of choice — OR meaningful content exists but is locked behind a gacha system, aggressive paywall, or grind so steep most players won't clear it through normal play. | The customize screen is there but either the choices don't matter or the content you actually want is out of reach. |
| 1 | No meaningful customization — each character has one fixed kart with no parts to swap, no visual options to change, no creation tools, and nothing to unlock through play. | What you start with is what you're stuck with. |
