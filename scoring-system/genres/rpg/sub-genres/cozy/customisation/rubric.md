# Metric: Customisation
**Genre:** RPG
**Sub-genre:** Cozy
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = decorating and building systems are deep and flexible enough that two players' spaces look nothing alike, 1 = customisation is limited to a handful of cosmetic presets

## Why This Matters

Creative expression is one of the clearest, most directly-named draws in the research file: Palia's research states plainly that "decorating and house building are excellent," and Stardew Valley's is framed as a replayability driver — "players can chose from many farm maps, so replayability is high," because each map forces a different approach to laying out the same buildings and crops. Both signals point at the same underlying thing: players don't just want to decorate, they want enough structural freedom that their choices actually look different from someone else's.

External sources back this with concrete scope. Palia's housing system allows up to 3,500 placed decor items on a single housing plot — a genuinely high ceiling for expression, not a token feature. Animal Crossing's "Designing" mechanic goes further structurally, covering not just furniture placement but a house's walls, flooring, and ceiling — meaning customisation extends to the shell of the space, not just what's inside it. That's the range this rubric is checking for: surface decoration is the floor, structural control over the space itself is the ceiling.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Customisation covers both furnishing (a large, varied item catalogue with free placement, not grid-locked) and structural elements — walls, flooring, exterior layout, or map/plot choice. The item ceiling is high enough (thousands of placeable pieces, as in Palia) that running out of room is a real planning constraint, not a limitation of the system. | You could spend as much time decorating as farming, and it wouldn't feel like a side activity. |
| 4 | Strong furnishing freedom and a decent item catalogue, but one structural layer is locked or limited (e.g. fixed exterior, or a handful of preset floor plans rather than free layout). | You'll make your space genuinely yours, with one or two things you wish you could change. |
| 3 | Decoration exists and items can be placed with some freedom, but the catalogue is modest and structural choices (walls, exterior, map layout) are minimal or absent. | Your space will look a little different from a friend's, but not dramatically so. |
| 2 | Customisation is mostly grid-locked placement from a small item set, or limited to choosing from a short list of preset rooms/skins rather than building up a look piece by piece. | You'll hit the ceiling of what you can change within your first session of decorating. |
| 1 | There's no meaningful decorating system beyond maybe a character skin or a single preset layout choice made once and never revisited. | Your space looks the same as everyone else's. |
