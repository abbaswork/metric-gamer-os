### Reddit Important Factors according to users
Brawlhalla:
- runs smoothly on most devices (either consoles, pcs, or low quality laptops)
- cross-platform 
- movement feels good 
- people don't like uneven splits of passive and aggressive players (e.g too many passive players that get rewarded)
- simple controls and no huge information wall
- the grind is rewarding
- It's extremely balanced at high ranks, which isn't really the case for a lot of other platform fighters
- It has a HUGE esports scene that the devs lean into
- It has tons of battlepasses and cosmetics and it's free
- It's arguably one of the fastest fighting games out there
- skill ceiling is high but not unobtainable 
- big roster 
- constant updates 
- easy to pick up, harder to master 
- weapon system -  Every character is unique because of the sigs and stats, but you always can always rely on the two weapons with light attacks
What makes a good fighting game:
- roster
- netcode 
- Universal vs Character specific mechanics
- diverse Defense options
- If you have assists, is it just functionally another swappable move?
- art style 
-  standout presentation and a lot of content 
- Freedom of expression and decision-making. Having various options for mechanics like movement and combos is super important
- satisfying gameplay (hard to describe. good animation and audio cues help), good art style and overall aesthetic, and good netplay.
- big the current player base is. I don't have much motivation to start playing a game if it seems dead.
- Control. Or atleast the illusion of it. It is not about the combos, it is not about the balance, it is not about the moves or options etc. Those come afterwards, what matters is: I, the user, must 'feel' that I have proper control of my character.
- Inputs shouldn't be hard for the sake of being hard
- Ensure charge and motion characters both exist 
- Neutral must feel interactive, and like I can actually maneuver around people just flinging them selves at me.
- 
Characters over systems, neutral over constant aggression, kits feeling like the entire thing can be used, rather than just a few moves being best. Balance helps a lot, because I hate picking an interesting character only to then realize that one is kinda booty.
- A readable screen that’s not so packed with details and effects
- The roster, the artstyle first. Many qol options (replays, tutorials, pinned combos, combo trials, if possible character tutorials), good netcode, rich content both singleplayer and multiplayer. Multiple modes, deep customization.
- sound design.
- Allowing newbs to play without making it feel like a punishment to vets while being deep enough to enjoy for years to come.
- If you don’t have good character designs and graphics most people won’t even try the game
