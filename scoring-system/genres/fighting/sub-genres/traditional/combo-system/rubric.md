# Metric: Combo System
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Traditional
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = expressive execution layer with accessible routes and high-end creative ceiling, 1 = execution barrier blocks competition or depth is absent

## Why This Matters

Combos are the execution layer unique to traditional fighters — the sequence of moves that converts a hit confirm into meaningful damage. The combo system determines the skill expression ceiling, the risk/reward of optimal routing, and whether execution difficulty is a meaningful differentiator or simply friction.

The debate around combo systems is specific and well-documented in the research. The accessibility side: "new players struggle to 'shoot their gun' at all and don't stand a chance against someone who can." Traditional fighter combos — particularly those involving tight link timing, input precision, and cancel windows — create a specific layer of execution barrier that has no equivalent in other genres. The satisfaction side: "landing airtight combos is fun" and "using motion inputs and landing airtight combos is fun for certain players who enjoy the mechanical practice aspect." The "little bit of dopamine" of execution registering correctly is real.

Street Fighter 6's approach represents the current design benchmark: multiple combo routes exist for the same character, accessible routes deal slightly reduced damage while advanced routes reward execution investment, and the gap between simple and optimal isn't wide enough to prevent competition across execution levels. Dragon Ball FighterZ introduced auto-combo systems that let new players convert without learning full routes — with the trade-off of limited damage ceiling.

The acceptance criteria for a well-designed combo system: accessible routes exist and are competitive (not just survival tools), optimal routes reward the investment without being so demanding they're impractical under match pressure, and the creative space between accessible and optimal is where most of the system's long-term skill expression lives.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Accessible combo routes let players convert hits reliably from the first few hours of play, with a meaningful but not punishing damage gap versus optimal routes. Optimal routes reward execution investment without requiring inhuman precision under match pressure — they're achievable with practice and pay off in meaningful damage. The creative space between accessible and optimal is large: players at the same competitive level can express individual style through routing choices, spacing, and conversion selection. New combo routes and extensions can be discovered post-launch as players explore the system. | You're converting from the start. A year later you're still finding new routes. The combo system has a ceiling worth chasing. |
| 4 | Good accessible routes with clear optimization paths. Some characters have richer combo systems than others. The gap between accessible and optimal is meaningful without being discouraging. Most players can execute their preferred route under match pressure with practice. | The system rewards investment. The execution skill layer is real and satisfying to develop. |
| 3 | Functional combo system with limited depth. Most characters have one primary route and minor variations — the creative space is narrow. Link timing is either so lenient it removes execution feel or so tight it discourages optimization. The gap between accessible and optimal play is narrow enough to reduce the sense of progression. | Combos work. They don't provide much of a reason to study them beyond learning the basics. |
| 2 | Combo execution is either so demanding that players rarely convert reliably under match pressure, or so simple that skill expression through routing is impossible. One bread-and-butter combo handles most situations and there's little incentive to learn variations. Accessible routes underperform enough that they're handicaps rather than realistic alternatives. | The execution barrier blocks play or the combo system is too shallow to engage with beyond the basics. |
| 1 | The combo system doesn't function as a skill expression layer. Either combos are too mechanically demanding to execute in real matches, or the system is so simplified that no expression is possible. The execution component adds frustration or boredom without reward. | The combo system is the game's weakest dimension and actively undermines competitive depth. |
