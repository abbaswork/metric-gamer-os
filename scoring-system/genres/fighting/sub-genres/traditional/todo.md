# Sub-genre: Traditional Fighter

Classic 2D or 2.5D one-on-one fighters with round-based health bars, motion inputs (quarter-circles, Z-inputs, charge moves), and strong emphasis on frame data, footsies, and match-up knowledge.

**Representative titles:** Street Fighter 6, Mortal Kombat 11, Dragon Ball FighterZ, Granblue Fantasy Versus: Rising, 2XKO, Killer Instinct, Dead or Alive

## Rubric Variations Needed

| Metric | Why a variation is needed |
|---|---|
| **Gameplay Depth** | Frame data, footsies, option selects, and zoning are concepts specific to traditional fighters. The genre rubric covers depth in general terms; a traditional rubric scores against these specific dimensions and what it means to master them. |
| **Accessibility** | Motion inputs are a specific barrier unique to traditional fighters. Modern input systems (SF6's modern controls, Granblue's simplified inputs) represent a specific design response worth scoring. The rubric should account for how well the game bridges classic and simplified control schemes. |
| **Competitive Balance** | The traditional fighter meta operates on frame-data advantages and character-specific match-up matrices at a level of granularity different from platform fighters. Tier gaps here are documented to the frame. |

Create rubric files here when a traditional fighter campaign is ready to run. Use the genre-level rubrics for any metric not listed above.
