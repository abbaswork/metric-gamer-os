# Metric: Roster Depth
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = visually distinct cast large enough that every player in a group develops a preference, 1 = so few choices or so little visual differentiation that character select is meaningless

## Why This Matters

Roster depth in party fighters serves a different function than in competitive ones. "The roster is the primary thing that draws players into a game — people need to be fans of the characters" (CritPoints). In the party context this means visual identification rather than competitive maining: players gravitate toward a character because of how it looks, not because of frame data or matchup charts.

The research is direct on this: "The roster, the artstyle first" and "good character designs and graphics — most people won't even try the game" (Reddit). The roster is the first impression the game makes on a group. A cast with strong, visually distinct designs gives every player at the table something to identify with before the first match starts.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Enough visually distinct characters that every player in a group can find one they identify with. Each character has a strong enough visual identity that players can form a preference based on appearance alone. No two characters look interchangeable. | Everyone at the table has a preference and can state why. The roster gives the group something to react to before the match starts. |
| 4 | Good visual variety with a few characters that feel like minor variations of others. Most players find someone they gravitate toward. Visual designs are clear and distinctive even if a small number are forgettable. | The roster works. Players develop preferences and stick to them. |
| 3 | Functional roster with limited visual variety. Several characters share enough visual similarity that preferences are harder to form. The roster is adequate without being a draw in itself. | The roster does not detract from the experience but does not contribute to it either. Character select is a formality. |
| 2 | Very limited roster or weak visual differentiation. Characters feel interchangeable enough that players struggle to form a preference. The group runs out of meaningfully distinct options quickly. | There is barely a choice to make. The roster is a weakness rather than a feature. |
| 1 | Minimal cast with insufficient visual differentiation for players to form preferences. Character select adds nothing to the experience. | The roster is an afterthought. |
