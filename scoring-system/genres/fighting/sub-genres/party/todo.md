# Sub-genre: Party Fighter

Physics-based or casual brawlers where chaotic local multiplayer fun takes priority over competitive depth. Skill expression is secondary to moment-to-moment chaos and social enjoyment.

**Representative titles:** Gang Beasts, Stick Fight: The Game, ROUNDS, Clone Drone in the Danger Zone

## Rubric Variations Needed

| Metric | Why a variation is needed |
|---|---|
| **Gameplay** | Party fighters intentionally sacrifice competitive depth for chaos. The genre-level Gameplay Depth rubric would score these games poorly by design. This sub-genre-specific rubric redefines gameplay quality as emergent moment generation — scored against six checklist items covering physics emergence, stage interactivity, stage variety, multi-player collision potential, match length, and spectator legibility. |
| **Competitive Balance** | Balance in a party fighter is about whether the chaos feels fair across different players, not whether tier lists are healthy. The scoring standard is different: you're asking whether the game consistently creates close, exciting moments rather than whether the meta is clean. |
| **Accessibility** | "Easy to pick up" is a hard requirement for the genre. The rubric should score how quickly a group of non-gaming friends can start having fun, not whether there's a training mode or combo trial system. |

Create rubric files here when a party fighter campaign is ready to run. Use the genre-level rubrics for any metric not listed above.
