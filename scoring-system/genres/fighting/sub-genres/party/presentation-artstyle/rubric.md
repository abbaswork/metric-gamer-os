# Metric: Presentation & Artstyle
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = visually expressive and readable under chaos with a distinct personality that draws groups in, 1 = bland or unreadable in a way that undermines the group experience

## Why This Matters

Presentation in party fighters carries extra weight because group entertainment begins before anyone touches a controller. The visual identity of the game — its characters, environments, and animation style — is the first thing a group sees, and it sets the tone for the session. Gang Beasts' gelatinous character designs and brightly coloured settings communicate "this is silly and physical" immediately. That communication is part of the game's value.

Screen readability under chaotic multi-player conditions is the critical functional dimension: when four players are fighting in a cramped environment with physics-driven animation flying everywhere, players need to be able to track their character and understand what's happening. Visual clarity during chaos is harder to achieve here than in any other fighting sub-genre because the chaos is the feature. The best party fighters manage this through strong character colour variety, clear environmental silhouettes, and animation that reads even when it's exaggerated.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | The game's visual personality communicates its premise immediately — the artstyle tells you this is a physics-based party brawler before you read a word of description. Characters are colour-coded and visually distinct enough that each player can track their character during four-player chaos. Animation is exaggerated and expressive in ways that make interactions funny to watch even from the outside. Environments are visually interesting and readable — the comedy of a collapsing stage or a moving hazard is enhanced by how clearly it's presented. | The game looks like the fun it promises. Anyone watching can follow what's happening and enjoy it. |
| 4 | Strong visual personality with clear character differentiation. A few characters or environments push visual density during chaos but the overall readability holds. The artstyle communicates the genre premise clearly. | The presentation delivers on its promise. A few moments of visual confusion don't undermine the experience. |
| 3 | Functional presentation with a clear enough artstyle. Characters are identifiable but don't generate strong visual personality. Environments are readable without being visually interesting. The game looks fine without communicating much about why it's fun. | The presentation does its job. It doesn't add to or subtract from the experience. |
| 2 | Character differentiation is weak enough that players occasionally lose track of themselves during chaos. The artstyle doesn't communicate the game's personality effectively. Animation doesn't enhance the comedy of interactions. | The presentation works against the genre. Following the action takes concentration that should be going toward enjoying it. |
| 1 | The visual presentation is so unclear or bland that tracking characters during multi-player chaos is genuinely difficult, or the artstyle is so off-putting that groups are reluctant to engage with it. | The presentation undermines the experience before the physics even get started. |
