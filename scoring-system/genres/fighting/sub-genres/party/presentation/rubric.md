# Metric: Presentation
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = visual identity communicates the game's premise at a glance and characters remain trackable during four-player chaos, 1 = premise is unclear from appearance alone or characters are indistinguishable during chaotic play

## Why This Matters

Presentation in party fighters carries a burden no other fighting sub-genre faces: the visual identity must sell the game to an entire group simultaneously, including members who don't play video games. Traditional and competitive fighters are chosen by individual players making an informed decision. Party fighters are chosen in a group context — someone sees the game on a screen and the group either decides to try it or moves on. "Looks fun" is the selection criterion and it must land in seconds. In the party fighter context, this means the visual style needs to communicate that the game is physical, accessible, and immediately silly without requiring text or explanation.

The second dimension is the spectator experience. In a party fighter, not everyone holds a controller at all times — the visual presentation must entertain people who aren't playing. Gang Beasts achieves both: "the bright and bold visuals make it easy for players to distinguish between characters and identify hazards within the arenas" while the gelatinous characters with "exaggerated movements and expressions are designed to evoke laughter" from players and observers alike. The visual design works at the controller and from the couch simultaneously.

The third dimension is functional: with four players fighting in close quarters under physics-driven chaos, characters must remain visually trackable. Research on multiplayer game character design confirms that distinct silhouette design accelerates target identification in dynamic environments. In a party fighter, losing track of your own character in a four-player pile removes the sense of authorship over what's happening — which is the genre's core promise.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | The visual style communicates the premise at a glance — a non-player seeing it for the first time can tell it's physical, accessible, and silly without text or explanation. Characters remain individually trackable through four-player chaos through distinct silhouettes and colour palettes. The animation and character design are visually entertaining to watch without a controller in hand. | The game sells itself from across the room. You never lose yourself in the chaos. | 4 | The visual style communicates the premise and characters are mostly trackable, though dense four-player moments occasionally make the action harder to read.
