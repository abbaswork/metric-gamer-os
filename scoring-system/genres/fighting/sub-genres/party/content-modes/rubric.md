# Metric: Content & Modes
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = map and mode variety gives groups genuinely different experiences across sessions, 1 = one mode on one map with nothing else

## Why This Matters

Players explicitly call for "multiple modes, deep customisation" and "rich content both singleplayer and multiplayer" in fighting games (Reddit). For party fighters, the content that most directly extends the game's life with a group is map and mode variety — the mechanisms that ensure sessions do not become repetitive.

Map variety is a documented driver of player engagement. "The principle of variety means that maps do not become monotonous and that players can explore multiple strategies and playstyles" and developers achieve this through "different terrain types, obstacles, and interactive elements" (MY.GAMES, level design research). Maps that differ only visually without changing how play unfolds do not satisfy this principle — the variety needs to be mechanical, not cosmetic.

Mode variety (teams, free-for-all, time-based, stock-based, special rules) gives groups agency over how they want to play in a given session and extends the range of experiences available within the same game.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Multiple maps with mechanically distinct dynamics — different terrain types, hazards, and interactive elements that change how play unfolds, not just how it looks. Multiple game mode variants let groups choose how they want to play. Both local multiplayer and single-player content are present and functional. | Groups can play across multiple sessions without the experience becoming repetitive. Each map creates genuinely different situations. |
| 4 | Strong map variety with a few that feel like lesser versions of others. Multiple modes exist though not all see regular use. Local multiplayer and single-player content both present. | The content holds a group for a good session. The variety is real even if some of it goes unexplored. |
| 3 | Adequate maps and modes but sessions start feeling repetitive. Maps create some variety without the terrain, hazards, or interactive elements changing meaningfully between them. Mode options are limited or feel like minor variations of the same format. | The game works for a session. Repeat nights start to feel the same. |
| 2 | Limited map and mode variety that repeats quickly within a single session. Maps are visually different without creating different play situations. Mode options are minimal. | Groups exhaust what the game offers within a short session. |
| 1 | One or two maps with a single mode. No meaningful variety. The game is exhausted in a single sitting. | There is nothing to come back to after the first session. |
