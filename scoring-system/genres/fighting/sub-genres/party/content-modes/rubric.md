# Metric: Content & Modes
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = rich mode variety and maps that give groups different experiences each session, 1 = one mode on one map with nothing else

## Why This Matters

Content in party fighters is primarily about map and mode variety — the mechanisms that ensure no two sessions feel identical. A party fighter with five maps that play completely differently creates more long-term entertainment than one with twenty maps that are slight variations. Mode variety (teams, free-for-all, last-player-standing with items, special rule variants) extends the game's shelf life within a group.

Single-player content and training tools are secondary in this sub-genre — a party fighter's primary audience plays in groups rather than alone. Online multiplayer matters but less centrally than in competitive games; the core use case is local. The content that matters most is whatever keeps a group of four players at the same screen entertained for an extended session without repetition setting in.

Post-launch support in party fighters tends to take the form of new maps, characters, or game mode rules rather than balance patches or competitive updates — and that's the right priority for the audience.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Multiple meaningfully different maps that create distinct session dynamics — not just visual variety but different environmental interactions, hazards, and layouts that change how play unfolds. Multiple game mode variants (teams, free-for-all, time-based, stock-based, special rules) let groups choose based on how they want to play. Local multiplayer is smooth and supports the expected player count without friction. Post-launch content has added new maps or modes that expanded the experience. | Groups can play for an extended session without the experience feeling repetitive. Each map visit feels like a slightly different game. |
| 4 | Strong map variety with a few that feel like lesser versions of others. Multiple modes exist though not all see regular use. Local multiplayer works well. Post-launch support has added something. | The content holds a group for a good session. The variety is real even if some of it goes unexplored. |
| 3 | Adequate maps and modes but the session starts feeling repetitive after an hour. Maps create some variety without dramatically changing the experience. Mode options are limited or feel like minor variations of the same format. | The game works for a session. Repeat nights start to feel the same. |
| 2 | Limited map and mode variety that repeats quickly within a single session. Most maps feel visually different without creating different play situations. Mode options are limited. | Groups exhaust what the game offers within a short session. |
| 1 | One or two maps with a single mode. No meaningful variety. The game is exhausted in a single sitting. | There's nothing to come back to after the first session. |
