# Metric: Content & Modes
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Party Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = map and mode variety gives groups genuinely different experiences across sessions, 1 = one mode on one map with nothing else

## Why This Matters

Party fighters are played in bursts — a few hours with a group, then another night a week later. The content has to sustain that pattern. Players are explicit about what they want: "multiple modes, deep customisation" and "rich content both singleplayer and multiplayer." For this genre, the content that most directly determines whether a group comes back is whether each session can feel meaningfully different from the last.

Map variety is the primary driver. The distinction that matters is mechanical, not visual — maps that look different without changing how play unfolds don't extend the game's life. When terrain, hazards, and interactive elements actually change the situations that arise, a group is still discovering new dynamics in their third session. When they don't, everything has been seen by the end of the first.

Mode variety gives groups agency over how a session runs — teams, free-for-all, time-based, stock-based, special rules — and local multiplayer is the primary context the genre is built around. Content that acknowledges how people actually play party fighters extends the game's range.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Enough map and mode variety that a group is still discovering different situations across multiple sessions. Maps create genuinely different play dynamics through terrain, hazards, or interactive elements — not just different visuals. Mode options give groups real agency over how a session runs. Local multiplayer is a first-class experience, and single-player content extends the game beyond group sessions. | Groups are still finding new situations in their third session. The game doesn't wear out. |
| 4 | Strong variety that holds a group across multiple sessions without becoming repetitive. A few maps feel like lesser versions of others, or mode options are present but some go unexplored. The content is real even if not all of it sees equal use. | The variety holds across a few sessions. Some of it goes unexplored but it's there when the group wants something different. |
| 3 | Enough variety to sustain a single session but repeat sessions start to feel familiar. Maps create some different situations without terrain or interactive elements changing meaningfully between them. Mode options are limited or feel like minor variations of the same format. | Works for a night. Repeat sessions start to feel the same. |
| 2 | The game's content is largely exhausted within a single session. Maps are visually different without creating different play situations. Groups run out of new things to discover before the night is over. | The game shows you everything it has in one sitting. |
| 1 | One or two maps with a single mode. Nothing to come back to after the first session. The game is seen in full the first time you play it. | There's nothing to come back to after the first session. |
