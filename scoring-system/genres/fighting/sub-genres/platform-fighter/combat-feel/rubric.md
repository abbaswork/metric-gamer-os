# Metric: Combat Feel
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Platform Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = aerial movement and impact feel like extensions of thought, 1 = the physics fight you before opponents do

## Why This Matters

Platform fighters are primarily aerial games — most combat happens off the ground, and the movement system is the game. Where traditional fighters evaluate combat feel through ground-level input responsiveness, platform fighters extend that question into three dimensions: jump arcs, aerial drift, dodge timing, wall mechanics, and the physics of being launched off stage. Brawlhalla's specific achievement is described precisely: "its best innovations being its jumps and wall movements — three jumps and nearly unlimited wall scaling make for fresh strategies and the buff in mobility means a lot of the high-adrenaline action happens off-stage."

The four feedback mechanisms from base fighting game research still apply but with platform-specific expressions:

**Hit stop:** In aerial contexts, hit stop determines whether a combo feels like controlled cause-and-effect or guesswork. More critically, a kill move's hit stop is the punctuation mark — the brief pause before the opponent is launched dramatically off screen is the equivalent of a perfect KO. That moment needs to feel earned.

**Audio:** The sound of a kill confirming — the opponent's stock-loss sound, the launch audio — is as important as any in-game sound. Brawlhalla players cite the satisfying audio-visual confirmation of an off-stage spike or a kill move connecting near the blast zone.

**Aerial physics as feedback:** Jump arc predictability, aerial drift responsiveness, and fastfall timing all serve as constant input-output feedback loops. When these feel correct, players learn quickly and feel in control. When they feel arbitrary, every off-stage death feels like the game's fault.

**Dodge recovery:** Air dodges and spot dodges in platform fighters carry their own feel — the invincibility window and the recovery frames after it need to be perceptible. A dodge that feels crisp and punishable rewards reading. A dodge with unclear timing creates confusion on both sides.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Characters have distinct weight and aerial drift that conveys personality without fighting your intent. Jump arcs are consistent and learnable across all jumps. Fastfall and aerial cancel inputs register cleanly. Hit stop on aerial connects is tuned so combo strings feel like controlled cause-and-effect. Kill moves produce a distinct, satisfying moment: brief stop, clear audio, unambiguous launch. Dodge recovery is perceptible enough that reading and punishing dodges feels fair. Off-stage movement feels like an expressive skill, not a punishment zone. | You stop thinking about the physics within hours. The game's aerial system becomes a language. |
| 4 | Strong aerial movement and feedback across most of the cast with one or two characters whose weight class or aerial drift doesn't match the overall standard. Kill move confirmation is satisfying in most situations. Dodge recovery is generally readable. A few edge cases where the physics don't respond predictably to inputs. | You'll feel in control quickly. The exceptions stand out because the baseline is so good. |
| 3 | Functional aerial movement that works without distinction. Characters feel similarly weighted and aerial drift doesn't vary enough to feel expressive. Hit stop exists but doesn't vary meaningfully between move weights. Kill confirms work without producing a strong moment. Off-stage feels mechanical rather than expressive. | The physics work. You'll never be stopped by them, but they won't pull you deeper either. |
| 2 | Aerial movement is inconsistent enough to undermine intention — drift is too strong or too weak to control reliably, or jump arcs behave differently in ways that can't be predicted. Dodge recovery is opaque enough that using dodges feels like gambling. Kill move feedback is weak or absent — you don't know a stock was taken until the respawn. | Off-stage deaths start feeling like the game's fault more than your own. |
| 1 | The aerial physics system is the primary obstacle. Jump arcs are inconsistent, dodge timing is unknowable, and kill move feedback doesn't confirm what happened. Every off-stage moment is unpleasant regardless of outcome. | The game fights back before any opponent does. |
