# Checklist: Gameplay Depth
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Platform Fighter

---

5 = Off-stage play is a genuine two-sided read rather than a formula, you can influence your trajectory when hit in ways opponents have to account for, platform positioning opens combo routes the other player has to respect, stock count changes how you both approach the match
4 = Clear depth in neutral and off-stage, edge-guarding has real counterplay on both sides, platform tech rewards learning, though a few situations feel predetermined where one option is too strong to contest
3 = Depth is present but the off-stage game doesn't fully deliver both ways, either recovering or edge-guarding rarely pays off, influencing your trajectory when hit doesn't feel like a perceptible skill difference in practice
2 = Off-stage play is effectively one-sided, stock count doesn't change how you approach the match, platform tech exists without producing meaningful differences in outcomes
1 = No meaningful layer beyond basic aerial hitting, off-stage is not a game state, what you know about the game doesn't affect what you can do, the skill ceiling arrives within hours
