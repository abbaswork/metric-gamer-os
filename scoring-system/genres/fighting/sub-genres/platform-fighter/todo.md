# Sub-genre: Platform Fighter

Games where combat takes place on platforms and victory comes from launching opponents off stage edges. Stock-based lives, aerial mechanics, and off-stage play are core to the experience.

**Representative titles:** Brawlhalla, Rivals of Aether

## Rubric Variations Needed

The following pool metrics need platform-fighter-specific rubric files because their scoring criteria differ meaningfully from the genre defaults:

| Metric | Why a variation is needed |
|---|---|
| **Combat Feedback** | Platform fighter movement involves jump arcs, aerial drift, fastfall timing, and recovery mechanics that don't exist in traditional fighters. The feedback signals here extend beyond ground-level input responsiveness to cover aerial physics consistency, kill confirmation distinctness, knockback direction readability, and dodge timing perceptibility. |
| **Gameplay Depth** | Edge-guarding, off-stage play, stock management, and aerial combo routing are core tactical dimensions unique to this sub-genre. The neutral game involves platform positioning and aerial approach angles not covered by the genre rubric. |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Platform fighters are distinguished by how hard they are to master, not just how easy they are to enter. The learning curve includes platform movement physics on top of standard fighting game concepts. Brawlhalla's specific achievement — "simple controls and no huge information wall" while maintaining a high skill ceiling — is a platform-fighter-specific design challenge worth scoring against. Named Skill Ceiling rather than Accessibility to reflect that the mastery dimension is the primary draw. |

Create rubric files here when a platform-fighter campaign is ready to run. Use the genre-level rubrics for any metric not listed above.
