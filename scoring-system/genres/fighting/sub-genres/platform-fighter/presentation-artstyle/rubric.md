# Metric: Presentation & Artstyle
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Platform Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = distinctive visual identity with screen clarity during aerial chaos and impactful kill confirmation, 1 = presentation actively hurts readability during play

## Why This Matters

Platform fighters place specific demands on visual presentation that traditional fighters don't: the screen needs to remain readable during multi-hit aerial exchanges, blast effects need to clearly communicate launch direction and power, and stages need enough visual clarity that platform edges are never ambiguous. "A readable screen that's not so packed with details and effects" is a core player concern — and in platform fighters, the chaos of four-player free-for-alls or off-stage exchanges makes this even more critical than in 1v1 traditional fights.

Kill confirmation is a platform-fighter-specific presentation moment: the stock-loss sound, the launch effect, and the stock indicator update form a visual-audio feedback package that needs to clearly communicate what happened and to whom. Brawlhalla's cosmetics ecosystem — skins registering 43 search interest — shows that when visual identity is strong, players actively invest in customising it. Platform fighters that earn cosmetic investment have presentation worth building on.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Strong visual identity across characters, stages, and UI — character silhouettes are distinct enough to read clearly even during multi-player aerial exchanges. Stage platforms are visually unambiguous — you always know where the ground is. Kill confirmation is a satisfying audio-visual moment: launch effect, sound, and stock indicator update all fire clearly and together. Cosmetic depth gives players something worth customising and showing off. | The game looks as good as it plays. You can read what's happening on screen even at its most chaotic. |
| 4 | Clear visual presentation with strong highlights. A few characters or stages push visual density high enough to cause occasional confusion in multi-player. Kill confirmation is generally satisfying. Cosmetic system is present and reasonable. | The presentation holds up. Rare confusion moments stand out because the baseline is clean. |
| 3 | Functional presentation. Characters are identifiable and platforms are readable under normal conditions. Kill confirmation works without producing a strong moment. No strong visual identity emerges but nothing actively distracts. | The game looks fine. Presentation doesn't add to the experience but doesn't undermine it. |
| 2 | Visual clutter during multi-player exchanges makes tracking characters and attacks genuinely difficult. Stage platform edges are sometimes ambiguous. Kill confirmation is weak enough that you don't always clearly know a stock was taken. | Chaotic moments become genuinely confusing. You spend attention on parsing the screen that should go toward playing the game. |
| 1 | Presentation actively hurts readability. Multi-player screens are unreadable during exchanges. Stage geometry is visually unclear. Kill feedback is absent or misleading. | The presentation is an obstacle. You can't read what's happening on screen. |
