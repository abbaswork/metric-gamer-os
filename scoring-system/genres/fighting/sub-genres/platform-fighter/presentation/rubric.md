# Metric: Presentation
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Platform Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = music, animation, and cosmetic system combine to give the game a strong identity players actively invest in, 1 = presentation is generic enough that the game leaves no impression outside of its mechanics

## Why This Matters

Some platform fighters operate as free-to-play live-service titles — Brawlhalla is the most prominent example. For these games in particular, the presentation layer is not just aesthetic decoration. It is how the game builds identity that players carry between sessions, and it is the primary vehicle through which players invest in the game non-competitively. A player who has spent time and attention on their Legend's skin, weapon skin, and podium is more attached to the game than one who has not. Brawlhalla skins "represent player identity, dedication, and sometimes rarity that commands respect" — the cosmetic system is the game's long-term relationship with its player base expressed visually.

Music contributes to this beyond individual matches. Brawlhalla's music is documented as "epic oriented, feeling like a gladiator waiting for your turn in the back rooms of the colosseum," with strong and calm moments that "even address storytelling." A game whose music is generic or absent misses the opportunity to build the atmosphere that carries a session.

Animation expressiveness matters because platform fighters have more animation states than traditional fighters. Characters spend time in aerial movement, wall mechanics, idles, and taunts — states that do not exist in a traditional fighter's ground-based neutral game. These states are where character personality lives. "Characters should give the impression of life through timing and expressiveness." A character with a distinctive idle, a readable personality through movement, and a satisfying taunt is a character players want to play — and want to invest in cosmetically.
