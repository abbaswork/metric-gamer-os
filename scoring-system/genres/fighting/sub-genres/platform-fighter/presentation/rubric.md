# Metric: Presentation
**Genre:** Fighting
**Sub-genre:** Platform Fighter
**Type:** Qualitative (observable signals)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = music, animation, and cosmetic system combine to give the game a strong identity players actively invest in, 1 = presentation is generic enough that the game leaves no impression outside of its mechanics

## Why This Matters

Platform fighters are almost universally free-to-play and live-service. This means the presentation layer — music, animation expressiveness, and the cosmetic system — is not just aesthetic decoration. It is how the game builds identity that players carry between sessions, and it is the primary vehicle through which players invest in the game non-competitively. A player who has spent time and attention on their Legend's skin, weapon skin, and podium is more attached to the game than one who has not. Brawlhalla skins "represent player identity, dedication, and sometimes rarity that commands respect" — the cosmetic system is the game's long-term relationship with its player base expressed visually.

Music contributes to this in a way specific to the sub-genre's session structure. Platform fighter sessions are longer than individual matches — players queue, spectate, and return to lobbies repeatedly. The audio environment shapes those gaps as much as the matches themselves. Brawlhalla's music is documented as "epic oriented, feeling like a gladiator waiting for your turn in the back rooms of the colosseum," with strong and calm moments that "even address storytelling." A game whose music is generic or absent misses the opportunity to build the atmosphere that holds a session together.

Animation expressiveness matters because platform fighters have more animation states than traditional fighters. Characters spend time in aerial movement, wall mechanics, idles, and taunts — states that do not exist in a traditional fighter's ground-based neutral game. These states are where character personality lives. "Characters should give the impression of life through timing and expressiveness" (Game Developer, 12 Principles of Game Animation). A character with a distinctive idle, a readable personality through movement, and a satisfying taunt is a character players want to play — and want to invest in cosmetically.

## Observable Signals

| Signal | What to check |
|---|---|
| Music quality and variety | The game has an original OST that contributes a distinct atmosphere; music varies meaningfully across stages or game modes; the audio identity is recognisable and associated with the game |
| Cosmetic system depth | Cosmetic options extend beyond colour swaps — skins, weapon skins, taunts, KO effects, or equivalent exist and allow meaningful individual identity expression; at least some options are earnable through play |
| Animation expressiveness | Characters have distinct idle animations and personality expressed through movement; aerial and wall-mechanic animations are varied across the cast; characters feel alive between active combat exchanges |

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | All three signals present and well-executed. The game has a distinct, recognisable audio identity — music sets an atmosphere that persists across the session, not just the match. The cosmetic system has genuine depth across multiple categories with at least a partial earn-through-play path. Characters have distinct animation personalities that make them identifiable by movement alone and give players a reason to invest in their mains beyond competitive function. | The game has an identity outside its mechanics. You are attached to your character and the atmosphere before you count wins. |
| 4 | Two signals strong, one underdeveloped. Most commonly: music and animation expressiveness deliver but the cosmetic system is shallow or entirely paywalled. Or the cosmetic system is deep but the music is generic and characters lack animation personality. | Presentation lands in most areas. The weak signal is noticeable but does not undermine the overall identity. |
| 3 | Signals present but none are strong. Music exists without being distinct or atmospheric. Cosmetics are limited to a small number of options without meaningful variety. Characters have functional animations without expressive personality states. | The game has a presentation layer but no identity. Nothing in the presentation gives you a reason to feel attached to it. |
| 2 | One or two signals present. Music is absent or generic. Cosmetic options are minimal or entirely locked behind payment with no earn path. Character animations are functional but identical in personality — characters feel interchangeable outside their movesets. | The game makes no impression outside its mechanics. Sessions feel disposable. |
| 1 | No signals present. Generic or absent music. No meaningful cosmetic system. Character animations express no personality. The game presents itself as a mechanical exercise with nothing surrounding it. | The presentation communicates nothing about why this game is worth returning to. |
