# Metric: Roster Monetisation
**Genre:** Fighting — Free Niche
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = full roster accessible without spending, 1 = most competitive characters are locked behind paywalls

## Why This Matters

In fighting games specifically, roster access is a competitive concern, not just a content concern. If the best characters require purchase, the monetisation model directly affects competitive viability — unlike cosmetics, which are purely aesthetic. Reddit flagged the underlying feeling: "Balance helps a lot, because I hate picking an interesting character only to then realize that one is kinda booty." In free-to-play games this pain is compounded when the viable characters are the paid ones.

Brawlhalla has established the benchmark. Each Legend costs 5,400 Gold (earned in-game) or 300 Mammoth Coins (premium currency). New Legends launch at 7,200 Gold for the first two weeks before dropping to standard price. A weekly free rotation of 8–10 Legends lets players trial characters before spending gold — the rotation changes every Wednesday. As a completely F2P player, one documented Diamond-ranked player unlocked the full roster in three months through ranked gold grinding. Full F2P unlock across the roster takes 8–12 months of regular play at average gold-earn rates (playbrawlhalla.com). The community perception: "the journey of unlocking is part of Brawlhalla's charm."

The model is the score-5 benchmark: purchased Legends give cosmetic variants (skins, alternate colours), not competitive access. Every character in the meta is earnable without spending.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Every character is accessible through in-game currency earned by playing. Premium currency or real money only purchases cosmetic variants (skins, alternate colours). Competitive viability is never paywalled. | Your main is never a purchase decision. The whole roster is a grind target, not a price tag. |
| 4 | Most characters are earnable through play with a small number available only through purchase. Paid-exclusive characters are not tier-defining and the free roster covers all competitive archetypes. | The paywall exists but it doesn't touch competitive play in a meaningful way. |
| 3 | Characters require either purchase or significant grind — free players can access the full roster but the time investment is steep. New characters launch behind a paywall for a period before becoming earnable. | You can get everyone eventually but the game makes you work for it or wait. |
| 2 | A significant portion of the roster requires direct purchase with no in-game currency alternative. Some of those characters are tier-relevant. Free players are working with a visibly limited pool. | Character selection is a spending decision as much as a preference decision. |
| 1 | Competitive characters are primarily or exclusively purchased. Free-to-play players have access to a token roster that doesn't cover the meta. Playing seriously without spending is not a real option. | The free-to-play label is misleading. Competitive play requires a roster that costs money. |
