# Metric: Ranked Mode
**Genre:** Fighting — Multiplayer Niche
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = ranked mode is a rewarding long-term progression system, 1 = ranked mode is punishing or absent

## Why This Matters

Brawlhalla ranked was one of the most searched queries in the research (44 search interest) — players investigate ranked before investing in a game. Reddit discussions confirm it drives long-term retention: "the grind is rewarding" in Brawlhalla is tied directly to ranked progression, not cosmetics. The esports infrastructure ("It has a HUGE esports scene that the devs lean into") creates stakes that make ranked feel meaningful.

Three conditions determine whether ranked works: "There must be a substantial player-base"; "The skill-based matchmaking system should ideally pair you against players of equal skill level"; and "The amount of latency and input delay should be minimal" (litetheironman.medium.com). When these fail, the cascade is fast — poor netcode shrinks the pool, matchmaking accepts wider skill gaps, rank stops reflecting actual skill.

The purpose of a well-designed ranked mode is specific: "ranked modes should be utilized as a tool where you can get consistent high-quality practice, with tournament brackets being where that practice pays off." When ranked design fails this, the psychological cost is documented: instead of learning, players fixate on rank movement — "Rather than trying to understand why they lost...their main takeaways are more emotional and subjective." Guilty Gear Strive's implementation is cited as the positive benchmark: floor-demotion mechanics that reduce rank anxiety without eliminating stakes, producing "a ranked mode that has been way more fun and addicting to play in than any other fighting game."

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Ranked mode has clear skill tiers with meaningful thresholds, consistent matchmaking within rank, and a reward structure that reflects genuine progression. Rank resets and seasonal structure keep it fresh. The ladder feels like an honest measurement of where you are. | Climbing is satisfying on its own terms. Each rank up feels earned. |
| 4 | Solid ranked structure with minor issues — slower matchmaking at extreme ranks, minor MMR inaccuracies, or reward structure that doesn't fully match effort. The core loop is rewarding. | Ranked works well. The gaps are noticeable but don't undermine the experience of climbing. |
| 3 | Functional ranked mode that feels inconsistent at the edges — wide skill gaps within ranks, slow matchmaking, or a reward structure that doesn't reflect time invested. Getting to high rank is achievable but the journey isn't particularly satisfying. | Ranked is a way to play the game without being a reason to play it. |
| 2 | Ranked mode has structural issues that undermine the progression loop — matchmaking puts widely different skill levels together frequently, rank doesn't reflect skill, or the reward structure makes climbing feel meaningless. | Ranked exists but doesn't function as intended. It can actively frustrate more than casual modes. |
| 1 | Ranked mode is absent, broken, or so poorly designed it's actively avoided by the community. | There is no meaningful competitive structure to participate in. |
