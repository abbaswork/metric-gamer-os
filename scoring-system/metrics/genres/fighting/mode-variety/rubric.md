# Metric: Mode Variety
**Genre:** Fighting (Single Player)
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = deep and varied single player content, 1 = effectively nothing outside of versus

## Why This Matters

The NeoGAF community thread on Tekken 7's single player experience was specific about what made it a failure: the removal of Survival, Team Battle, and Ghost Battle compared to prior entries. "They even removed Survival, Team Battle, and Ghost Battle which made the previous games worth playing." Arcade mode was four fights. The community expectation wasn't a cinematic story — it was the collection of modes that gave a single player reasons to keep coming back. Mortal Kombat 11's Towers of Time, Street Fighter 6's World Tour, and Tekken 8's Arcade Quest and Ghost Battle are the reference points for what single player mode investment looks like. The flip side — games that ship without these modes and expect players to sustain interest through versus — is a documented community complaint at launch across multiple franchises.

## Sources

- **NeoGAF: "Tekken 7 is the worst single player experience I've ever had in a fighting game"** — players specifically cite removal of Survival, Team Battle, and Ghost Battle as the core failure; "Arcade mode is like only 4 fights" (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **ResetEra: "Are there other fighting games with impressive story modes like the recent Mortal Kombat games?"** — MK11's Towers of Time and unlockable content cited as the bar for single player investment (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/Fighters and r/MortalKombat** (training data) — launch mode count is a consistent talking point in reviews and community threads; players compare available modes directly against prior entries in the same franchise
- **r/StreetFighter** (training data) — SF5's launch content was widely criticized; SF6's World Tour mode and Battle Hub were praised as a correction

**Reference points:**
- Tekken 7 launch: weak arcade ladder, no survival, no ghost battle → community backlash
- MK11: Towers of Time, arcade ladder with endings, training, online → praised for depth
- SF6: World Tour, Battle Hub arcade cabinets, combo trials, training with full frame data → praised
- Tekken 8: Arcade Quest, Ghost Battle, Treasure Battle, Tekken Ball, full training suite → praised
- GGStrive: Arcade, survival, mission mode, training → functional but not exceptional

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | A full suite of single player modes beyond arcade — survival, challenge or mission modes, a dedicated single player campaign or world tour, ghost or CPU battle with customisable settings, and training mode with frame data and combo trials. Unlockable content is tied to meaningful engagement across multiple modes. | You'll find different things to work on every session. The game gives you reasons to keep returning that don't require another player. |
| 4 | Solid mode selection covering most of the expected set. Arcade ladder with character-specific endings, training with useful data, and at least two additional modes that offer real depth. One or two modes from the full set are missing or thinner than expected. | There's enough here to stay engaged solo for a long time. A few modes feel undercooked but the core selection holds. |
| 3 | A functional but basic selection — arcade ladder, training mode, and maybe survival. The game covers the minimum without going further. Nothing feels neglected but nothing stands out either. | You can practise and run through arcade. After that, the solo content starts repeating itself quickly. |
| 2 | Arcade ladder and training mode only. No additional modes and limited unlockable content tied to single player. | The arcade ladder runs out fast. Training mode is useful but it's not a mode — it's a tool. |
| 1 | Effectively no single player content beyond versus. Arcade mode is either absent, a handful of fights with no endings, or training mode is the only solo option. | If you don't have someone to play with, there isn't a game here. |
