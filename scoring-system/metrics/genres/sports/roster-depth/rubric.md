# Metric: Roster Depth
**Genre:** Sports
**Type:** Quantitative + Qualitative (licensing breadth + accuracy)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = comprehensive real-world licensing with accuracy, 1 = fully generic

## Why This Matters

Playing as your actual club against a real rival is categorically different from playing as "Blue City FC" against "Red United." Licensing is one of the defining purchasing decisions in sports gaming, and the community and the industry both treat it as such.

Football Manager operated for years without the Premier League licence, forced to name the competition the "English Premier Division" and represent most clubs without their official kits or logos. In FM24, only Brighton and Manchester City had accurate visual representation. When Sports Interactive finally secured the Premier League deal for FM25, studio director Miles Jacobson described it as something the studio had "always wanted" — the years without it are documented as a meaningful gap in the product, not a minor detail. The community response to unlicensed content in football games is consistent: option files and custom kit packs are among the most-visited community threads in PES and eFootball forums, built specifically to replace generic stand-ins with real club identities. The effort players invest in recreating licensing confirms how strongly they value real-world representation.

The licensing gap is not only about football. EA Sports withdrew its college basketball game proposal in 2023 after schools chose to license with 2K instead, with the licensing decision effectively determining which publisher got to make the product. The college football licensing battles that preceded the EA Sports College Football 25 release required individual player agreement — 134 FBS programmes needed to be brought in. These are not legal formalities. They are commercial decisions that define whether a sports game is the real thing or an approximation of it.

The secondary layer is accuracy within licensing. A game that holds the Premier League licence but ships with incorrect kits, outdated squads, or ratings that do not reflect current form underdelivers on what the licence promises. Roster accuracy at launch and the quality of post-launch roster updates are consistent community evaluation criteria across football, basketball, and any sport with real-world participant data to reference.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Comprehensive real-world licensing across all major leagues, teams, or participants relevant to the sport. Player likenesses, ratings, kit accuracy, and stadium representation are precise. The database reflects real-world rosters at launch and receives post-launch updates to account for transfers and form changes. | You will find your club, your players, and your ground represented correctly. The game feels like the sport it is simulating, not a legal approximation of it. |
| 4 | Strong licensing covering most major competitions with some gaps. Player and team accuracy is high in covered content. One or two significant leagues or participants are missing or represented partially — the equivalent of FM having the Premier League but not all kits correct. | You will have your main clubs and most of the players you care about. There are gaps, but the majority of what you would want is present and represented well. |
| 3 | Partial licensing. The most prominent competition or teams are present but coverage drops significantly below that tier. Some clubs or athletes are represented with generic names or kits despite being real-world entities. The gap is noticeable to anyone who follows the sport at more than a surface level. | You will find the biggest names but miss a significant portion of what the real sport includes. If your club is outside the top tier, you may be playing as a stand-in. |
| 2 | Minimal licensing. A handful of real teams or players are present as marquee names while the majority of the roster is generic. The real-world names present feel more like marketing than genuine coverage. | The licensed content is a surface layer over a generic product. Most of the game will feel disconnected from the actual sport. |
| 1 | No real licensing. All teams, players, and venues are fictional or generic. There is no connection to real-world clubs, athletes, or competitions. The option file community cannot help because the gap is too wide. | You are playing a sports game, not a version of the sport you follow. |
