# Metric: Gameplay
**Genre:** Sports
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = controls feel precise and authentic to the sport, 1 = controls feel arbitrary and disconnected

## Why This Matters

Gameplay is the core question of any sports game: does playing it feel like the sport? This covers controls, responsiveness, physics, and whether the game's chosen approach — simulation or arcade — is executed with internal consistency. The community distinction that surfaces across sports gaming discussions is not realism vs. fun. It is whether the controls translate intention into outcome. When they do not, no other quality in the game compensates.

The failure modes the community documents with the most specificity are input delay and animation lock. EA SPORTS FC 24 players documented "at least a 1 second delay on inputs, which makes the game very difficult to play" in Steam forums. Undisputed, the boxing game, received sustained complaints about "huge input delay in movement and animation lock preventing fluid motions" — players were forced to wait for animations to complete before they could act again, which is a specific mechanical failure that disconnects input from outcome. eFootball players described "no responsiveness — players stop for a moment and don't react." In EA SPORTS FC 25, reports of "players running in the opposite direction despite player input" appeared across community forums during launch. These are not complaints about difficulty. They are complaints about control systems that do not reliably execute what the player instructs.

The praise patterns are the mirror of those complaints: games where inputs translate immediately, where skill learned early carries through to harder content, and where the mechanics reflect what the sport actually demands from a participant.

## Sources

- **EA SPORTS FC 24 Steam Community discussions** (live source, confirmed via web search) — players documented "at least a 1 second delay on inputs, which makes the game very difficult to play"; the thread attracted widespread agreement
- **EA SPORTS FC 25 Steam Community discussions** (live source, confirmed via web search) — players reported "players sometimes running in the opposite direction despite player input"; controls described as overcomplicated
- **Undisputed (boxing) Steam Community discussions** (live source, confirmed via web search) — "huge input delay in movement and animation lock preventing fluid motions, with players having to wait for animations to complete before responding" documented across multiple threads
- **eFootball Steam Community discussions** (live source, confirmed via web search) — "no responsiveness, blocking of controlled players who stop for a moment and don't react for a second or two" cited as the defining frustration
- **r/FIFA and r/NBA2k** (training data) — "scripted" outcomes and momentum-shifting mechanics are consistent community complaints; praise threads consistently cite responsiveness and skill reward as the defining qualities

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Controls are precise and immediately responsive with no meaningful input delay. Inputs translate to on-field outcomes consistently across all match situations. The game's mechanics — whether simulation or arcade — follow clear internal logic. Skill learned early transfers to high-level play with no animation lock breaking the feedback loop. | What you intend to do and what happens are the same thing. You will feel the difference between a well-timed tackle and a mistimed one, and you will know why. |
| 4 | Controls are responsive with minor inconsistencies in specific situations. Core mechanics are well-implemented. The occasional input feels slightly off — a short delay in a transition, an animation that runs fractionally long — but the execution holds up across most of what the game asks of you. | You will notice occasional moments where the game does not quite do what you expected. They will not define your experience but you will remember them. |
| 3 | Controls work but feel generic or slightly disconnected from the sport. Key mechanics are present but not fully realised. The game functions without feeling tuned specifically to this sport. Some inputs take a moment longer than expected and you adjust your timing accordingly. | The game is playable and fair. You will get into it, but it will not feel like the sport in the way the best games in the genre do. |
| 2 | Controls are inconsistent or unresponsive in regular play. Input delay is noticeable enough to affect decisions during normal gameplay. Animation lock limits what you can do in consecutive moments. Inputs do not reliably translate to outcomes and the gap is wide enough to cause regular frustration. | You will be working around the controls rather than playing through them. Mistimed inputs due to delay will cost you in situations where skill should have been enough. |
| 1 | Controls feel arbitrary. No consistent relationship between input and outcome. Input delay or animation lock is severe enough to make the core game loop unworkable. The mechanics bear no meaningful resemblance to the sport being simulated. | The game does not function on a basic level. Skill has no consistent effect on what happens. |
