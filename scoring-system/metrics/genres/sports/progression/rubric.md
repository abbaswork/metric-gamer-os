# Metric: Progression
**Genre:** Sports
**Type:** Qualitative + Quantitative (community consensus + career depth)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = rewarding long-term progression with real consequence, 1 = no meaningful progression

## Why This Matters

Progression in a sports game determines whether you are still playing three months after launch or whether you shelved it after a weekend. The primary vehicle is career mode — managing or playing through seasons, developing players or squads, and facing a structure where decisions carry forward into subsequent matches. When that structure is missing or thin, the research is consistent: community engagement collapses quickly and the game gets shelved.

The eFootball case is the clearest documented example of total progression failure. Players described the game as having "nothing to do beyond Dream Team" — no master league, no become a legend, no league mode. When the co-op mode was later removed, the community described it as "the last tolerable part of the game." What remained was an online transaction loop with no progression structure underneath it. The community was not complaining about the quality of career mode. They were documenting its complete absence.

EA SPORTS FC's career mode represents the stagnation pattern — the mode exists but receives no meaningful development across iterations. notebookcheck.net described it as having been "long criticized for its lack of meaningful updates" and noted it "barely features in trailers." FIFA 20 drew enough community pressure over career mode quality that goal.com ran coverage of it being "slammed for being unplayable." The specific complaints were structural: unrealistic starting squads, a fixture system that felt random, and press conferences that served no mechanical purpose. These are not polish failures. They are evidence that the mode was not given resource to develop as the rest of the game was updated around it.

Undisputed's career mode failure was more specific. A player with 25+ years of gaming experience described it as "one of the worst career modes among all sport games" — the core complaint being that the game kept no record of who you had fought or what the results were. A career mode without historical records is not a career mode. It is a series of disconnected matches with a menu between them.

This is the default 4th metric for Sports. When the sub-genre is Co-op / Multiplayer, this metric is replaced by Multiplayer Quality, since multiplayer-focused titles are evaluated on the competitive experience rather than the solo career structure.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Career or franchise mode spans multiple seasons with meaningful player or squad development, consequential decisions that carry forward across seasons, and a results history that is tracked and accessible. Development systems — transfers, youth programmes, player ratings, season objectives — give each season its own shape. | You will still be mid-career six months after launch. Decisions you make in season one will matter in season three, and you will know it at the time. |
| 4 | Solid career mode covering multiple seasons with decent development systems. Progression feels rewarding with some depth limitations. Objectives exist and provide direction. The long-term structure holds up with some repetition creeping in past the second or third season. | Career mode will carry you through a significant number of hours before it starts to feel routine. The second season has enough variation to justify continuing. |
| 3 | Functional career mode covering multiple seasons without meaningful development or consequence. Objectives are present but routine. Each season plays out similarly to the last. Match history is tracked but does not feed into anything. | You will get a full season's worth of play out of it. By season two or three, the repetition will be apparent and the reasons to continue will be thin. |
| 2 | Career mode is shallow or partially implemented. It exists across matches but development systems are absent or cosmetic. The mode does not track meaningful history or give decisions forward consequence. The gap between what is promised by a career mode and what is delivered is wide enough to feel like a missing feature. | Career mode will hold your interest for a week. After that, each session feels disconnected from the ones before it. |
| 1 | No meaningful progression system. Equivalent to eFootball with the single-player modes stripped out — a product built around an online transaction loop with no career structure underneath. Matches are standalone and history is untracked. | There is no reason the tenth session should feel different from the first. |
