# Metric: Game Modes
**Genre:** Sports
**Type:** Qualitative + Quantitative (community consensus + mode count)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = comprehensive mode variety with genuine depth, 1 = exhibition only

## Why This Matters

A sports game with only exhibition matches runs out of reason to return to it quickly. The modes a game ships with determine whether it sustains a player through a weekend or through a season. The community consistently separates games into two camps: those that feel like complete products with career modes, tournaments, and skill-building options, and those that feel like tech demos with matchmaking bolted on.

The most documented failure case in sports gaming is eFootball's 2021 relaunch. Players documented the game as having "no master league, no league mode, no become a legend — absolutely nothing for single mode." The co-op mode that remained was later removed, described by players as "the last tolerable part of the game." When that went, the assessment was that there was "nothing to do in eFootball beyond Dream Team." This is the benchmark for the bottom of this metric — a game technically shipping but offering nothing for a player who wants to engage with the sport outside of online transactions.

EA SPORTS FC's career mode represents the stagnation failure pattern rather than the absence one. notebookcheck.net's coverage of the FC 26 reveal noted career mode had been "long criticized for its lack of meaningful updates" and that it "barely features in trailers." FIFA 20 drew enough community anger over career mode quality that goal.com ran coverage of it being "slammed for being unplayable", with a follow-up piece on changes made in response. The failure pattern here is a mode that exists but that the development team has visibly deprioritised across multiple yearly iterations.

Undisputed's career mode was described by an experienced player in Steam discussions as "one of the worst career modes among all sport games" in 25+ years of gaming. The specific complaint was structural: players could not view their fight history or cumulative results anywhere in the game. A career mode without records of what you have done is not a career mode in any meaningful sense.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Comprehensive mode set covering career or franchise mode with meaningful depth, a full tournament or cup structure, quick play options, and at least one additional mode — skill challenges, historic scenarios, or mini-game variants. Career mode includes multi-season progression with player or team development and decisions that carry forward. Records of results and history are tracked and accessible. | You can approach this game multiple ways and sustain any of them for a significant amount of time. Career mode alone will carry you past the 20-hour mark. |
| 4 | Strong core modes with some gaps. Career or franchise mode is present and functional across multiple seasons. Tournament structure exists. Quick play is covered. One or two expected modes are missing or thinner than comparable titles. | The main reason you would buy this game is covered. Some of the extras you would expect are not there or feel lighter than the genre norm. |
| 3 | Covers the basics without depth beyond them. Career mode spans multiple seasons but without meaningful development or consequence. Tournament and quick play modes are present. No standout additional modes. Results may be tracked but carry little forward weight. | You will get a decent amount of play out of the core modes before they feel repetitive. Do not expect to still be in the career mode after a month. |
| 2 | Limited modes with shallow content. Career mode is thin or effectively absent. Quick play is the primary option. What is present functions but does not give you a reason to return to it. The absence of fight or match history makes long-term engagement hollow. | You will run out of meaningful things to do quickly. The game feels like a slice of what a full release should be. |
| 1 | Exhibition only, or online-only with no meaningful single-player structure. No career mode, no tournament structure, no progression across matches. Equivalent to eFootball at its worst — a technical product with no game around the mechanic. | There is nothing holding sessions together. You will have seen everything meaningful within an hour. |
