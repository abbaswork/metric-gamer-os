# Metric: Track Variety
**Genre:** Racing
**Type:** Quantitative
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = most unique locations, 1 = fewest

## Why This Matters

In a NeoGAF open letter addressed directly to racing game producers, players ranked track variety as more important than car roster size. That's a strong community signal. Going through the same tracks on repeat was described as one of the defining reasons people stop playing a game, with specific criticism of developers building "tracks that are just variations on the same track in the same environments." Variety here means unique real-world or fictional locations — reverse, wet, and layout variants of the same circuit do not count separately.

## Sources

- **NeoGAF: "An open letter to racing games producers"** — fans explicitly ranked track variety above car lists; repetitive track design called out directly (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **NeoGAF: racing game complaints thread** — "going through the same tracks, over and over" cited as a primary reason for abandoning games (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/patientgamers** (training data) — long-term value discussions use track repetition as the main dividing line between games worth sticking with and those that wear out their welcome
- **Steam user reviews** (training data) — "not enough tracks" is among the most common complaints in racing game reviews

## Rubric

Scored on unique track locations. Reverse, wet, and alternate layouts of the same location do not count separately.

| Score | Threshold | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 30+ unique locations | You'll be racing somewhere new for quite a while before anything starts to repeat. Familiarity fatigue won't come up unless you're putting in serious hours. |
| 4 | 20–29 unique locations | There's enough variety to stay interested for a good while. Repetition only becomes a conversation once you're deep into the game. |
| 3 | 12–19 unique locations | A few hours a week and you'll be fine. Commit to longer sessions and the tracks start feeling familiar sooner than you'd want. |
| 2 | 6–11 unique locations | You'll know every track well within a few hours. Extended time with the game makes the limited selection hard to ignore. |
| 1 | Under 6 unique locations | You'll have the circuits memorised in a session or two. After that, track repetition isn't a background issue anymore. |
