# Metric: Handling Model
**Genre:** Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = highly realistic and distinct, 1 = cars feel identical and physics are disconnected

## Why This Matters

A large car roster means little if every car handles the same. The handling model determines whether the variety in a game's lineup is actually felt behind the wheel or just cosmetic. This metric scores three things: how distinct cars feel from each other, how handling responds to different terrain types, and how closely the physics reflect realistic driving behavior. A game can be arcade or simulation — the question is whether the model delivers on what it's supposed to be.

## Sources

- **simracingcockpit.gg** — community discussion confirming that handling physics are "always a hot topic" and that players hold strong, divided opinions that define their loyalty to specific games (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/simracing** and **r/racing** (training data) — players consistently describe car distinctiveness and terrain response as defining qualities of a good handling model; games where cars feel the same across classes are a recurring complaint
- **r/patientgamers** (training data) — handling realism is frequently cited as the reason players return to specific racing titles over others
- **Steam user reviews** (training data) — "on rails", "all cars feel the same", and "terrain doesn't change anything" are consistent complaints; "each car feels different" and "you feel the road" are consistent praise

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Cars handle distinctly from each other in ways that reflect their class and specification. Handling shifts noticeably across different terrain types. Physics respond to inputs in ways that mirror real driving behavior. | Each car you try has its own character. Surface changes affect how you approach a corner, and what you know from one car doesn't fully transfer to another. |
| 4 | Most cars feel distinct with some exceptions. Terrain variation influences handling in most conditions. Physics are mostly consistent with realistic expectations. | You'll notice real differences between most car classes. Some surface transitions feel slightly flat, but the model holds up across most of what the game offers. |
| 3 | Cars within the same class feel similar to each other. Terrain variation has a limited effect on handling. Physics work but don't closely reflect realistic behavior. | You'll get used to one car and find others in the same class feel much the same. Surface changes don't demand much adjustment from you. |
| 2 | Most cars handle similarly regardless of class or type. Terrain has minimal impact on behavior. Physics feel disconnected from what driving should feel like. | Cars blur into each other quickly. You'll drive on gravel the same way you drive on tarmac. |
| 1 | No meaningful distinction between cars. Terrain has no effect on handling. Physics feel arbitrary. | There's no reason to adjust your driving for the car you're in or the surface you're on. |
