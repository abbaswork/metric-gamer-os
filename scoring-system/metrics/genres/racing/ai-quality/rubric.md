# Metric: AI Quality
**Genre:** Racing
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = AI problems absent, 1 = AI is the main frustration

## Why This Matters

Rubber banding is one of the most consistently complained-about mechanics in racing game communities. Players don't just dislike it — they describe it as actively dragging down games that are otherwise strong. A second complaint surfaced in research adds another dimension: difficulty that punishes a single mistake so heavily that you lose an entire race, turning challenge into frustration rather than something worth overcoming. Good AI creates moments that feel earned. The NeoGAF community put it directly: "finishing in 5th place coming from 20th is better than starting in 1st and winning without any action."

This is the default 5th metric for Racing. When the sub-genre is Split Screen, this metric is replaced by Split Screen Performance, since you are racing human opponents and AI quality becomes irrelevant.

## Sources

- **HowToGeek** (community-sourced analysis) — "poorly implemented rubber banding is unfortunately common in the genre and drags down otherwise fantastic racing games" (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **NeoGAF: "An open letter to racing games producers"** — "finishing in 5th place coming from 20th is better than starting in 1st and winning without any action"; race design that punishes single mistakes called out directly (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **NeoGAF: racing complaints thread** — difficulty that becomes "very difficult, very fast" combined with "a mistake often means you lose the entire race really kills it" (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/forza** (training data) — Drivatar system sparked community debate about AI that learned poor player behaviour
- **r/granturismo** (training data) — AI criticised across entries for feeling scripted rather than reactive

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | The AI actually delivers some competitive racing experiences. Competitors battle for positions, show unique behaviors, make the occasional mistake, and there is a smooth progression in difficulty. | You'll know that you won those races on merit and not because of scripting; close races feel intense, not predetermined, and increasing difficulty is logical. |
| 4 | Good AI with a few flaws. There may be some rubber banding, or the AI will sometimes behave in an unusual way, but it's still a competitive racing experience. | The AI opponent may occasionally snap into catching up with you, but for the most part, racing will remain an enjoyable experience. |
| 3 | Functional but uninteresting AI behavior. Competitors will be competitive at the appropriate difficulty level, but they follow predictable patterns and rarely surprise you. | You can have exciting races provided the difficulty levels are set correctly; but you'll still likely figure them out in about three hours of playtime. |
| 2 | Issues arise between the AI and the player community. The problem with rubber banding is huge, the opponent's actions are unpredictable, or difficulty seems to have two options: very easy for normal mode and nearly impossible for the hard one. | You'll probably spend time managing the AI's problems rather than just racing. |
| 1 | The AI is considered by many to be broken. Rubber banding occurs heavily, opponents drive directly into you, or the races feel simply predetermined regardless of how you play. | What makes it difficult to enjoy the game is the very AI itself. |
