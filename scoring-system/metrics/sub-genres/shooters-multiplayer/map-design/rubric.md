# Metric: Map Design
**Genre:** Shooters (Multiplayer)
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Delight — 5 = maps that create varied and replayable encounters, 1 = maps with structural problems that break competitive integrity

## Why This Matters

Map design in multiplayer shooters determines whether the game keeps producing new situations or collapses into the same patterns every match. The community values maps that reward positioning and game knowledge, where creative routing shifts the tempo of a match and multiple approaches remain viable. The complaints that surface are specific: spawn trapping that lets one team lock the other into their starting zone, single dominant routes that make play predictable, and sightlines that reward stationary camping over movement. Counter-Strike's Dust2 is the community reference for enduring map design. Over twenty years of play haven't exhausted its strategic depth because the three-lane structure creates distinct roles and decision points without making any of them mandatory.

The inverse is documented too. Call of Duty community threads regularly identify specific maps as broken because of spawn logic that puts players directly in front of enemies, or sightlines so long that one position dominates an entire map.

## Sources

- **gamedesignskills.com: "FPS Game Design"** — "multiple points of attack for enemy outposts and breach points that can lead to objectives; creative players can find new routes that completely shift the tempo of a match" cited as the design standard (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **Reed Kolbe survey of 106 FPS players** — map design ranks as a key satisfaction driver across all shooter sub-genres; tactical shooter players rate maps as highly as gun design (live source, confirmed via web search)
- **r/GlobalOffensive and r/Valorant** (training data) — map pool debates are among the most common community discussions; specific maps get removed or reworked based on documented structural complaints; Dust2 is the community's standard reference for good map design
- **r/CallOfDuty** (training data) — spawn logic and sightline complaints are the most common map-related discussions; specific maps praised or criticised for their structural approach

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Maps support multiple viable approaches built around different playstyles. Positioning and routing knowledge produce real advantages. Spawn placement doesn't hand one side a structural edge. Every map in the active pool produces varied encounters across matches. | You'll approach different maps differently. Knowing the map creates an edge, and that edge is worth building. |
| 4 | A solid map pool where most maps produce varied encounters. A couple of maps have minor flow issues — a sightline that leans slightly in one team's favour, or a route that sees more traffic than intended. Nothing that breaks how the match plays out. | You'll have preferences. No map will make you want to leave the lobby. |
| 3 | Functional maps that don't produce major problems. Encounters cluster in the same areas each match. The maps work without rewarding deep positioning or routing knowledge. | You'll know exactly where the fights are going to happen within a few matches. After that, the maps stop being interesting. |
| 2 | Maps with clear structural problems. Spawn traps are possible and repeatable, or a single route funnels almost all traffic, or stationary positions dominate sightlines. Exploiting the map's flaws becomes more important than playing the objective. | A few maps will actively frustrate you. The flaws change how the whole match plays out. |
| 1 | Map design breaks the game. Spawn trapping is routine and difficult to escape, or the pool is so small that every match feels settled before it starts. | The map is doing more work than the players. Where you spawn determines the match. |
