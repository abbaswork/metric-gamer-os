# Metric: Multiplayer Quality
**Sub-genre:** Sports — Co-op / Multiplayer
**Type:** Qualitative (community consensus)
**Direction:** Complaint — 5 = multiplayer problems absent, 1 = multiplayer is the defining frustration

## Why This Matters

Online sports games are built around competitive play, and the quality of that experience is determined almost entirely by the underlying multiplayer infrastructure. Connection stability, matchmaking fairness, and whether the online mode covers the same content as the offline game are the three pillars the community consistently evaluates. A sports game with solid gameplay and a broken online mode is, for the multiplayer-focused player, a game that does not work.

The most specific recent documentation of this failure is College Football 26, which experienced widespread desync errors during its early access period in 2025. Operation Sports reported that the problem occurred when "the player-owned client is out of sync with the game's servers, causing incorrect information to display during gameplay" — a player might see a pass dropped on their screen only to have it register as a completion. One player's tweet summarised the community experience directly: "Every CFB26 game I've played so far has been a desync." The root cause was servers unable to handle the volume of simultaneous early access players. The result, according to Operation Sports, was that online play was "extremely difficult for some and virtually impossible for others."

Rematch, Sloclap's arcade football game, faced a similar issue at a different scale. PC Gamer's coverage summarised the problem plainly in its headline: "If you catch the ball in Rematch, there's a small chance you didn't." The developer reported desync occurring in approximately 30% of games during testing across Steam and Xbox, and the problem persisted after full launch. In a game where a caught pass determines whether a play succeeds, 30% desync is not a background issue. It is a structural failure in the core mechanic.

WWE 2K24 documented desync issues persisting through patch 1.03, confirming that when multiplayer infrastructure fails, it does not always resolve quickly. NBA 2K players have documented disconnects mid-match that return them to the main menu without recording a result, which in competitive modes translates to a loss of progress earned across an entire match.

## Rubric

| Score | Description | What This Means for You |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Online and local multiplayer both function reliably across all main modes. Connection is stable with no meaningful desync during normal play — what happens on your screen matches what the server records. Matchmaking is fast and pairs players at comparable skill levels. Local co-op covers all modes available in solo play without feature gaps. | You can play online or with someone on the same couch and neither experience will remind you that you are playing a game rather than a sport. |
| 4 | Multiplayer works well with occasional issues. Connection is stable in most matches with isolated desync in high-traffic moments. Matchmaking is generally fair with some outlier pairings. Local co-op is present but may exclude one or two modes available in solo play. | You will get good multiplayer sessions regularly. The odd connection issue or mismatched opponent will not define your experience but you will encounter them. |
| 3 | Multiplayer is functional but inconsistent. Connection quality varies between sessions. Matchmaking is slow or produces mismatches at certain skill levels. Local co-op is present but limited in scope compared to solo play. Some sessions will run well. Others will be clearly affected by infrastructure issues. | You will learn which modes work reliably online and gravitate toward those. The inconsistency is manageable but you will be aware of it. |
| 2 | Significant multiplayer issues that affect most sessions. Connection problems are frequent rather than exceptional. Desync is common enough that match outcomes are regularly affected by what the server records rather than what the player did — the College Football 26 early access pattern. Matchmaking produces consistent mismatches. Local co-op has meaningful feature gaps or performance issues. | The multiplayer experience will feel like a lottery. You will have good sessions but you will be aware that the infrastructure is working against you as much as with you. |
| 1 | Multiplayer is effectively broken. Desync or server failures occur at the rate Rematch documented — roughly one in three games — or worse. Dead player pools make matchmaking non-functional. Local co-op is absent or performs so poorly it is not a practical option. The community has documented the multiplayer as the primary reason not to play the game. | The mode that defines this type of sports game does not work. |
