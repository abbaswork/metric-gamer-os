# Metric Gamer Pillars

## Pillar 1: Gamer sentiment is #1
Every page, campaign and strategy must be reviewed from the persona of a genuine gamer.

## Pillar 2: Transparency is the product
The methodology isn't a footnote — it's the reason to trust the scores. Rubrics are public, sources are named, every score traces back to a documented reason. This is the structural difference from every other ranking site.

## Pillar 3: Niche depth over broad coverage
The product is specific ranked lists for specific gaming contexts, not comprehensive coverage of everything. Being definitive for "best split-screen co-op PS4 RPGs" is more valuable than competing with IGN. The specificity of a Metric Gamer list is what makes it worth reading.

## Pillar 4: Community evidence, not editorial opinion
Scores reflect documented player sentiment from Steam, Reddit, and forums — not a single critic's view. The score reflects how the community actually experienced the game.

## Pillar 5: Monetisation must earn its place
Revenue keeps the platform running and Metric Gamer will pursue it without apology. But monetisation that compromises Pillar 1 is self-defeating — if gamers stop trusting the scores, there's nothing left to monetise.
