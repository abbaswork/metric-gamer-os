# Metric Gamer — Contributor Revenue Share Framework

This document governs how revenue and milestone payouts are distributed across the Metric Gamer team. It applies to all contributors from the date it is agreed and signed off by both owners.

---

## Ownership Structure

| Role | Share | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Owner 1 | 35% | Fixed. Never diluted by contributor performance. |
| Owner 2 | 35% | Fixed. Never diluted by contributor performance. |
| Contributor Pool | 30% | Earned. Distributed by points. Maximum 20% per individual contributor. |

The 70% owner split is permanent and does not change regardless of how contributor points are distributed.

---

## Contributor Pool Mechanics

There are up to 3 contributor slots. Each slot carries a **base allocation of 10%** of net revenue.

- A contributor who completes 100% of their allocated work earns their full 10%.
- A contributor who absorbs incomplete work from another slot can earn up to a **maximum of 20%** — they cannot exceed this regardless of how many additional tasks they take on.
- Any contributor slot that goes unfilled or unearned **reverts to the owners**, split equally between Owner 1 and Owner 2.

**Example:**
> Contributor A earns 1,000 points (full base). Contributor B earns 500 points (half their base). Contributor C completes nothing. C's unclaimed work was absorbed by A — A now holds 1,500 points. At payout: A earns $15, B earns $5, C earns $0. The unclaimed $10 (C's full base that no one absorbed) reverts to the owners.

---

## Point System

Points are assigned to each child OKR at the start of every campaign. Completing a task earns you its points. Not completing it means you do not earn those points — and they transfer to whoever picks up the task.

| Points | Share of base 10% allocation |
|---|---|
| 100 | 1% |
| 500 | 5% |
| 1,000 | 100% (full base allocation) |
| 1,500 | 150% (absorbed 50% of another slot's work) |
| 2,000 | 200% — hard cap, equals the 20% maximum |

Point values per child OKR are set by the owners at the start of each campaign and published before any work begins. Contributors must know their point allocation before agreeing to take on a task.

---

## Campaign & Deadline Rules

All work is tied to a **campaign** with a defined **go-live date**.

- Contributors must submit completed work **1–2 days before the go-live date**. This gives owners time to review before publishing.
- At the go-live date, owners publish whatever is done. Nothing waits.
- Any task not submitted by the deadline is considered incomplete. Its points are immediately available for another contributor to claim by completing it.
- If no one claims an incomplete task before go-live, those points revert to the owners.

**There are no extensions.** The go-live date is fixed. Work submitted after it does not earn points for that campaign — it may be carried into the next campaign at the owners' discretion.

---

## Payout Tiers

Payouts happen at defined milestones, not on a fixed monthly schedule.

### Tier 1 — Flat Milestone Payout

**Trigger:** Total site clicks across all channels reach **500 per 28-day window** (measured in GA4).

**Payout:** A flat pool of **$30 total** ($10 per contributor slot), funded by the owners and distributed by points earned across all campaigns up to the milestone date.

- A contributor who earned 1,000 points receives $10.
- A contributor who earned 700 points receives $7.
- A contributor who earned 1,500 points (absorbed another's work) receives $15.
- Any unearned portion reverts to the owners.

This payout is funded by the owners directly — it is not contingent on AdSense or any other revenue being live. It exists to make the system real and credible from the first milestone.

### Tier 2 — Revenue-Based Payouts

**Trigger:** Net revenue from all monetisation channels (AdSense, affiliate, sponsorships, licensing) generates a 30% contributor share that **exceeds $10 per active contributor slot** in a given period.

Once Tier 2 is active, payouts switch from flat to revenue-based:

- Payout = 30% of net revenue for the period
- Distributed proportionally by points earned in the campaigns that ran during that period
- Frequency: per monetisation milestone (not monthly — milestones are defined at the start of each quarter)
- "Net revenue" means gross platform income minus direct operating costs (hosting, tools, contractor fees). It does not include owner salaries or personal expenses.

Tier 1 and Tier 2 do not overlap. Once Tier 2 is active, Tier 1 flat payouts are retired.

## Transfer Rules Summary

| Situation | What happens to the points |
|---|---|
| Task completed on time | Contributor earns the points |
| Task not submitted by deadline | Points are open — any contributor can claim by completing it |
| No one claims the task before go-live | Points revert to owners |
| Contributor earns over 1,000 points | Points count up to the 2,000 (20%) hard cap; excess is ignored |
| Contributor slot is unfilled | Full 10% base reverts to owners |

---

## Agreement

This framework applies from the date both owners agree to it. Contributors must be shown this document and verbally or written-confirm they understand the point allocation for their tasks before any campaign begins.

Point allocations for each campaign's child OKRs are set and published by the owners in the relevant OKR document before work starts.
